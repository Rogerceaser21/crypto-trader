1 · How a peak is measured

  1. The week opens Sunday 17:00 NY. At that moment the weekend box freezes: the range of 15m closes from Friday 17:00 to Sunday 17:00 NY. This week: 63,878.2 – 64,869.1.
  2. The early window runs Sunday open → Tuesday 17:00 NY. Each closed bar updates the running window high and low.
  3. A peak candidate exists when the running extreme pokes ≥0.10% beyond the box — above 64,934.0 or below 63,814.3 this week (the dotted commit lines on the chart below). Poke above → SHORT side; below → LONG side; if both sides have poked, the bigger excursion wins and the other side gets nothing.
  4. The commit is provisional — on purpose. Every new extreme re-commits the machine and resets the trigger chain. Causally nobody can know "the" peak early; the tested edge (+0.347R × 167) always trades the current extreme.
  5. Only Monday peaks are tradeable (checked at entry time against the committed extreme's 5pm-NY trading day).

4 · When a peak is NOT painted — every reason

  • Price is inside the weekend box (or the box hasn't frozen yet on a brand-new chart load — the first loaded week has no Fri–Sun history, so it can never paint). Most of the time, this is the reason.
  • The poke is under 0.10% beyond the box. Real example circled on the chart above — a wick over the box top that never reached the commit line.
  • The other side is bigger. A genuine over-threshold poke paints nothing if the opposite side's excursion is larger — Monday's −0.22% below-box poke on the chart above lost to the +0.33% high and stayed invisible.
  • The bar hasn't closed yet. An intrabar wick over the line paints nothing until the 15m bar confirms (schematic in section 2).
  • Throttle (display only): the machine re-commits on every new extreme, but a new label only prints on the first commit, a side flip, or a move ≥0.25% beyond the last label. The chart below shows last week's slide: 18 commits, 9 labels — the small circles are commits the throttle kept silent (the chain still reset on each one).
  • The early window closed. After Tuesday 17:00 NY no new extreme commits, ever — the whole-week chart below shows last week's actual weekly high (65,590, Wednesday) that never got a label.
  • The week is decided. Once an entry fires (or a chain ends past Friday 08:00 NY), commits stop for the week even inside the window — last week nothing painted after the Tue 01:15 entry.

5 · How a committed peak becomes a trade

  1. L1 vector: the first candle after the peak that is a 200%-class PVSRA vector (volume ≥ 2× the average of the prior 10 bars) whose body crosses the 15m 50-EMA in the fade direction — for a SHORT: a red vector opening at/above the EMA (tolerance 0.10%) and closing at/below it. A red vector already below the EMA does not count — it must CROSS. Paints the yellow L1 box at its own bar, at close.
  2. Retest: a later bar that (a) wick-touches the EMA (tolerance 0.15%), (b) keeps its body on the trade side, and (c) price has returned into the L1 candle's range since L1. Abort if any bar closes back across the EMA (0.10% tolerance) or 320 bars pass. Paints the blue retest tag at its own bar.
  3. Entry = the next 15m open. Stop = running weekly extreme ±0.05%. Target 2.5R (input 1.9/2.5/3.0). Entries until Friday 08:00 NY. Paints the entry label (peak price quoted in the text) and forward stop/TP lines.

  • Signals untouched: V3.2 display diff vs the tested G1 build = zero signal-state writes; the +0.347R backtest, 95.2% mirror fidelity and the Tue 01:15 acid test describe these exact files.
