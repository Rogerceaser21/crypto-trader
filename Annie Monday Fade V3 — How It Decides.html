1 · How a peak is measured

  1. The week opens Sunday 17:00 NY. At that moment the weekend box freezes: the range of 15m closes from Friday 17:00 to Sunday 17:00 NY. This week: 63,878.2 – 64,869.1.
  2. The early window runs Sunday open → Tuesday 17:00 NY. Each closed bar updates the running window high and low.
  3. A peak candidate exists when the running extreme pokes ≥0.10% beyond the box. Poke above → SHORT side; poke below → LONG side; if both, the bigger excursion wins.
  4. The commit is provisional — on purpose. Every new extreme re-commits the machine and resets the trigger chain to it. Causally, nobody can know "the" peak before the week plays out; the tested edge (+0.347R × 167) is the version that always trades the current extreme. The gray peak? labels are exactly these commits (throttled to ≥0.25% steps so trend days don't spam).
  5. Only Monday peaks are tradeable (the validated edge). The gate is checked at entry time against the committed extreme's 5pm-NY trading day.

2 · How a committed peak becomes a trade

  1. L1 vector: the first candle after the peak that is a 200%-class PVSRA vector (volume ≥ 2× the average of the prior 10 bars) whose body crosses the 15m 50-EMA in the fade direction — for a SHORT: a red vector opening at/above the EMA (tolerance 0.10%) and closing at/below it. A red vector that is already below the EMA does not count — it must CROSS.
  2. Retest: a later bar that (a) wick-touches the EMA (tolerance 0.15%), (b) keeps its body on the trade side, and (c) price has returned into the L1 candle's range at least once since L1. Abort if any bar closes back across the EMA (0.10% tolerance) or 320 bars pass without a retest.
  3. Entry = the next 15m open after the retest completes. Stop = the running weekly extreme ±0.05%. Target = 2.5R (input: 1.9 / 2.5 / 3.0). Entries allowed until Friday 08:00 NY.
