S6 & S7 — Jewel Lite Scalps: crack attempt & backtest

Task KA4. Two questions: (A) can we rebuild the proprietary Krown “VMP / Jewel Lite” Fast oscillator from candles? and (B) what do the S6 (long) / S7 (short) rules do when backtested on the best line we can build? In-sample 2019-09-09 → 2024-12-31 only — the 2025–26 out-of-sample window stays untouched.

🟡 INCONCLUSIVE · PROXY

No edge on the proxy — but the proxy isn’t the real indicator.

The Jewel Fast line is proprietary; the crack attempt failed the bar (best replica corr 0.973, not 0.999, and it reproduces only ~1/3–1/2 of the true S6/S7 entries). Backtested on that proxy, both S6 (expR −0.31, PF 0.57, t −14) and S7 (expR −0.52, PF 0.37, t −40) are decisively negative in every grid cell — the average trade just bleeds the ~0.16% round-trip cost. This cannot condemn the real strategy: it is a mechanical sanity check on a proxy, not a verdict on Krown’s indicator. S6/S7 stay effectively blocked until Igor exports the raw Fast line or its Pine source.

⚠ PROXY LINE (corr 0.973) — NOT the real strategy. Every number, table and card below is computed on a double-EMA-smoothed price stochastic that approximates the Jewel Fast oscillator. It is not bar-exact, does not reproduce the indicator’s warm-up, and mis-times more than half of the real entry signals. Treat the results as “the rules, run on the closest line we could build” — nothing more.

A · The crack attempt (Part A)

Attempt #1 (CALIBRATION.md “V3”) reached correlation 0.88 and declared the base oscillator a proprietary-smoother MISMATCH. This attempt went wider — exploiting every exported column (Fast, JewelP, Percentile, DI-, DI+, ADX, White, Cyan, Blue) and ~1,700 candidate constructions across 10 smoother families.

What we found

  • Best replica — corr 0.973 (a real lift over 0.88): a double-EMA-smoothed stochastic of close. 
    Jewel-Fast PROXY = EMA( EMA( stoch(close, high, low, n=10), 5 ), 7 )
    stoch = 100 · (close − lowest(low,10)) / (highest(high,10) − lowest(low,10))
  • Still not the indicator. Two hard disproofs: (1) warm-up mismatch — the true Fast emits its first value at bar 34, but the corr-optimal fit emits at bar 19; every construction that warms up at ~34 collapses to corr 0.79–0.95. (2) A 0.97-corr line still gets the signals mostly wrong (below).
  • DMI block: exported DI+/DI- first-valid @14, ADX @27 → lengths pinned at 14/14, but standard Wilder DMI isn’t bar-exact on the export (a “DMI Factor 1” internal variant). Not on the S6/S7 path (the entry rides on Fast).
  • Colour decode (bonus, values-only): every bar is exactly one of White/Cyan/Blue. Cyan = Fast rising (99.4%), Blue = Fast falling, White = a turning bar (89% agreement). ⇒ the rulebook’s “first tick that closes white” entry = the Fast line turning.

C · Interpretations & conventions (every judgement call, declared)

  1. Jewel line = PROXY (jwl.jewel_fast_proxy, corr ~0.973). Thresholds ride on the Fast oscillator (task spec; the on-chart line is the 0–92 oscillator with guides 35.5 / 67.2 / 77.8). Attempt #1 mused the thresholds might ride on the JewelP percentile instead — unresolved, but immaterial since the base line is proxy either way.
  2. “Closes white” = the Fast line turns on bar t (S6: turn up in the <35 zone; S7: turn down in the 67–78 zone). Decoded from the exported colour flags (89% agreement). Causal: uses t, t−1, t−2.
  3. Entry at the next bar’s open + slippage (the signal is only known at the close of t). One position at a time; new signals ignored while in a trade.
  4. Stop = close beyond the range extreme (lowest-low / highest-high of the prior range_lb bars) per the rulebook’s “closed below range lows”; realised loss may exceed the reference stop (a close can gap past it). A 3% intrabar catastrophic backstop bounds tails (the rulebook’s “emergency wick”).
  5. TP = PMARP crossing its threshold on close (S6 >75, S7 <20), frame-verified DEFAULT PMARP (VWMA 20, lookback 350). Trade requires PMARP defined at entry.
  6. Ambiguous = LOSS. If a stop-close and a TP-close fire on the same bar, the stop (loss) wins.
  7. Sizing 1% risk of $10k, size = risk / stop-distance, stop-distance floored at 0.20% of price to avoid div-by-tiny leverage when entry sits on the range edge. Per-trade stats are size-invariant.
Venue note. The VMP indicator export is Bybit BTCUSDT.P; the backtest candles are Binance BTCUSDT-perp (the project’s 3.6M-row 1m set). The proxy line is computed from Binance candles here. Because the crack failed on either venue (the base is proprietary), this cross-venue detail changes nothing material. The 2025–26 out-of-sample window was never loaded (asserted in code).
