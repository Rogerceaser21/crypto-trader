The Combined Weekend Play — Backtest

Ride the weekend long, then flip into a Monday counter-trade at a declared trigger. Does the two-leg structure beat each piece alone and the validated Monday fade?

Verdict

❌ No robust value added — keep the simple validated Monday fade.

Three of the four flip triggers fail outright. Bolting an SD-level fade (F1) or an HPDR-band fade (F4) onto the weekend ride makes it worse than just riding — both fade legs lose or wash, and cutting the long short at the tag throws away drift. F3 is just "ride to Monday open," no flip.

Only F2 (the validated M/W fade) clears the mechanical t ≥ 2 bar (+198.8R, t = 2.43) — but it fails the spirit of the test. Its extra return over the fade is unhedged weekend bull-market beta: the always-long leg alone is +104R but only t = 1.22 (not established), lost −51R in 2022, and carries a 62%-of-account drawdown. Stapling the fade on top raises total R yet lowers the risk-adjusted quality (t drops 2.98 → 2.43) and multiplies drawdown ~10×. The one clean, regime-robust edge in this whole study remains the standalone validated fade: +0.382R, 91 trades, t = 2.98, max-DD 4.6R.

Weekend leg = ALWAYS-LONG, entered Fri 17:00 New York (Phase 4b found no credible weekend direction rule — the long is a decree, not a signal). 9 rows tested → treat any single t with multiple-testing caution.

The structure

What the combined play actually does

Every weekend you buy at the Friday close and hold long through the weekend (LEG 1 · RIDE). On Monday a trigger fires "breakout complete": you close the long and flip into a fade back toward the weekend base (LEG 2 · FADE). If nothing triggers, the long simply runs to the Monday-window close.

F1 and F4 fail cleanly. Both combined totals (+70R, +81R) are below leg-1-alone (+104R): the fade legs lose or wash (F1 fade −1.9R; F4 fade +0.3R over just 46 tags) and, worse, closing the long at the tag surrenders weekend drift. The paired test confirms it — adding the fade to the ride is significantly negative (F1 t −1.22, F4 t −1.03).

F2 is the only interesting case — and the honest read is "no." Yes, +198.8R clears t = 2.43. But dissect it:

  • +104R of it is just the weekend long (leg 1) — an unvalidated, undirected bull-market bet (t = 1.22, not established).
  • +34.8R is the validated fade — the real edge, already available standalone at a higher t (2.98) and one-tenth the drawdown.
  • The remaining ~+60R of "synergy" is a timing artifact: on spike-up Mondays the long exits near the spike top (before the fade-back) and then shorts the drop — a genuine double-win, but it only exists in one direction.

Honesty box

What could make these numbers lie — and what we did about it

  • Leg-1 R is not risk-based, it's notional-based. The weekend long has no natural stop, so we size it at a fixed $10k notional (1×) and define 1R = a 1% move. That makes leg-1 and leg-2 R's summable in dollars, but leg-1 R is far higher-variance — a single weekend can swing several R. The 62%-of-account max-DD on leg-1 alone is the honest cost of that.
  • The weekend edge is bull-market beta, not alpha. Leg-1 by year: 2020 +37R, 2021 +38R, 2024 +54R — but 2022 −51R. It is long-BTC-in-an-uptrend. Out of a bull regime it can flip negative; its t = 1.22 says we cannot even establish it in-sample.
  • F2's high total R buys lower statistical quality. Combined t (2.43) is below the fade-alone t (2.98) and drawdown is ~10× larger (45.6R vs 4.6R). More money, worse risk-adjusted — the opposite of what "adds value" should mean.
  • Multiple testing. Nine rows / four triggers were examined. A lone t = 2.43 that is itself driven by regime beta is not a bankable discovery — the pre-registered, independently-validated fade (t = 2.98) is.
  • Costs applied everywhere, ambiguity penalised. Taker 0.05%/side + 0.02% slip on every fill including the flip (both legs charged, mildly conservative); same-minute stop-and-target counts as a loss; all triggers and HPDR bands are strictly causal (HPDR uses only the previous completed 15-minute bar). F1/F4 fades reuse the frozen Phase-3 cell; F2 fade is read verbatim from the validated weekly-trades file.
  • In-sample only. Everything here is 2019-09 → 2024-12. Nothing was run on the locked 2025-26 block.
What this means for you

Bottom line, in plain English

  • Don't trade the two-leg combined play. The "ride then flip" structure does not create a robust new edge. On three of four triggers it is worse than doing nothing clever; on the fourth it only looks good because it smuggles in an unhedged long-BTC bet.
  • Keep trading the standalone Monday fade. That is the single clean, pre-registered, regime-robust edge in this whole line of work: +0.38R per trade, ~91 setups over six years, t ≈ 3.0, and only a 4.6R drawdown. It does not need the weekend ride bolted on.
  • If you want the weekend-ride return, know what you're buying. The +104R from holding long over weekends is real but it is market beta in a bull run, not a skill edge — it lost 51R in 2022 and can draw down 60%+ of the account. That is a position-sizing / regime decision, entirely separate from the fade, and should never be dressed up as part of a "strategy" t-stat.
  • The SD levels and the HPDR band are not useful flip triggers. The SD-level fade loses (Phase 3 already showed this); the HPDR 95% band tags too rarely and washes to zero. Neither improves on the fade.
