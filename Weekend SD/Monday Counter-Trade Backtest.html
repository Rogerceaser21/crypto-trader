Every knob was frozen before looking at results, all 48 combinations were run, and nothing was added afterwards. The honest read is blunt: the mechanical version of this trade loses money in every configuration, and the variants that lose reliably (21 of 48 are statistically significant) all lose — none win.

1 · How the trade works

Each Monday (02:00 → Tuesday 00:00 Dubai) two fade levels sit around the weekend base — one above, one below — placed at mean + k·SD of the historical Monday move. Whichever level price tags first is faded back toward base: a tag of the upper level is a short, a tag of the lower level is a long. Stop sits further out; target is base or the halfway point. One trade per weekend.

4 · Honesty box

Multiple testing. Running 48 variants, pure chance would be expected to throw up roughly 2 that look significant (|t| ≥ 2) in a random direction. Here we got 0 positive variants and 21 significant ones at |t| ≥ 2.5 — all of them negative (21/21). So this is not a case of “maybe one lucky cell”; the losing is systematic. Pooling all 3,738 trades gives -0.300R each (-1121R total).

Same-minute ambiguity. When a stop and target could both be hit inside one 1-minute bar, the trade is booked as a loss (never assume the good fill came first). Weeks where the up-level and down-level tagged in the same minute would be skipped as ambiguous — that happened 0 times across all cells (the two levels sit far enough apart in price that they never trigger in the same minute).

Untradeable variants. 4 of the 48 cells can't be traded at all: the k=2 · p95 stop cells for the 1-month and confluence sources — at two SD out the entry is already beyond the 95th-percentile level, so a p95 stop would sit inside the entry (nonsensical). They are:

  • k=2, 1-month (4 wk), Base target, p95 stop
  • k=2, 1-month (4 wk), Halfway target, p95 stop
  • k=2, Confluence, Base target, p95 stop
  • k=2, Confluence, Halfway target, p95 stop
The same k=2 · p95 combination for the 3-month and all-history sources is only “tradeable” on a handful of freak heavy-tail weekends (N of 1 and 9) and is not read into.

No look-ahead. Every level for a given weekend is built from completed prior weekends only — the expanding all-history estimate uses all weekends before this one (minimum 20-weekend warm-up), never the full-sample constants, so early weekends can't borrow the future. Trailing windows use the previous 13 / 4 weekends. A weekend's own move never touches its own levels.

5 · What this means for how Igor trades it today

The SD level by itself is not an edge. Mechanically fading every Monday tag — any distance, any lookback, any target, any stop — loses money after costs. The seductive part (a ~64% hit rate) is exactly the trap: the fade wins small and often, then a runaway Monday hands back more than a dozen wins in one shot.

So if the manual version has felt like it works, the edge is almost certainly in the parts a backtest can't see — which tags you skip, the higher-timeframe context you require, and how you cut or add by feel — not in the level. Two concrete takeaways:

  • Don't automate it as a standing rule. There is no set-and-forget version here that survives costs.
  • If you keep trading it manually, treat the SD level as a location, not a signal. It tells you where price is stretched; it does not tell you the fade will pay. The money has to come from a filter on top (trend regime, confluence with other structure, only fading into a level that also lines up with something) — and that filter is the thing worth testing next.
  • The recent years are the worst. 2023–24 were the biggest losers, so any “it worked for me” memory is more likely 2020–21 selection than a durable pattern.

Methodology

  • Definitions reused, not recomputed. Weekend base, Monday window and the UP/DOWN move distributions come straight from the Phase-1 weekend_observations.csv (277 clean weekends).
  • Data. 2.80M one-minute BTCUSDT-perp bars, 2019-09-09 → 2024-12-31 UTC, in-sample only. Nothing after 2024-12-31 is loaded — the 2025–26 block stays locked.
  • Costs. 0.05% taker fee per side, 0.02% adverse slippage on every fill including stops and market exits; fixed 1R = $100 on a $10k account, no compounding. Conventions taken verbatim from bt_engine.py.
  • Fills. Entry is limit-style at the level; stop / target checked bar-by-bar on 1-minute candles from the entry bar onward; same-minute both-touched = loss.
  • Determinism. Pure function of the inputs, stable sorts, no randomness. Two consecutive runs produce byte-identical sd_fade_cells.csv and sd_fade_trades.csv (md5 verified).
