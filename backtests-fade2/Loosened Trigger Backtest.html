Loosened L1 Trigger — Backtest

Does Igor’s 2026-07-13 rule loosening — the L1 PVSRA vector no longer has to body-cross the 15-minute 50 EMA, it only has to be a vector candle in the fade direction — help, hurt, or leave the validated Monday fade unchanged? The 50-EMA retest requirement and every other rule are held fixed. In-sample only (2019-10-20 → 2024-12-31, 62 months); the locked 2025-26 out-of-sample window is untouched.

Verdict

LOOSENING BACKFIRES — it does not add trades, it HALVES them. Keep the EMA-cross baseline.

The validated Monday fade (EMA-cross required) is 91 trades, 54.9% WR, +0.382R, PF 2.05, t=2.98, total +34.8R. Dropping the 50-EMA body-cross from the L1 vector shrinks that to 45 trades / +22.0R total (200%-class) and 30 trades / +17.8R total (150%-class) — half the trades or fewer, a third to half of the total R gone, and significance weakening from t=2.98 to 2.52/2.50. The mechanism is mechanical, not statistical: the loosened condition is a strict superset of the baseline test, so it always fires at or before the baseline L1 — typically right at the spike extreme, where price is still on the wrong side of the 50 EMA — and the unchanged abort rule (a close on the wrong side voids the setup) then cancels the week before any retest can form. Verified: 100% of the lost baseline weeks die by that early abort. The genuinely NEW entries the loosening buys are almost nonexistent and worthless: 1 new, -1.03R total (200%) and 2 new @ -0.01R avg (150%). Per-trade quality of the surviving weeks does rise (+0.489R / +0.594R, WR 60%/67%) — the accidental "immediate-rejection" filter keeps the best fades — but it comes nowhere near paying for the lost weeks. Keep the cross requirement. And if the intent of the 2026-07-13 ruling was MORE setups, this implementation cannot deliver that: the loosening only widens the net if the abort rule gets a companion ruling (e.g. don't arm it until price first reaches the 50 EMA) — that is a question for Igor, not something this test was allowed to invent.

3. What the loosening actually does — it narrows, it does not widen

The intuition behind the loosening was a wider net: fewer conditions on the L1 vector, more setups. The mechanics do the opposite. The loosened condition is a strict superset of the baseline candle test (it is the baseline minus the EMA clause), so it always fires at or earlier than the baseline L1 — verified across all 266 in-sample weeks with a baseline L1, zero violations (backtests/bt2_verify_mechanism.py). And earlier is worse: the first fade-direction vector usually prints right at the spike extreme, where price is still on the wrong side of the 50 EMA, so the unchanged abort rule (a close on the wrong side voids the setup) cancels the week before any retest can form — 100% of the dropped baseline weeks die exactly this way, within about three hours of the loosened L1. The marginal-trade ledger below shows the result: almost no new entries, many dropped weeks.

The survivor effect, honestly stated. The weeks that survive the loosening are the ones where the fade rejected immediately (price got through the EMA fast) — a genuinely stronger subset, which is why per-trade expectancy and win rate rise in the table of §2 while total R collapses. That is an interesting filter observation, but it is not what the ruling asked for, it is in-sample only, and with n=45/n=30 it is far too thin to trade over the 91-trade anchor.

5. Method, invariants & reproducibility

  • Only the L1 line changed. Direction (Sun/Mon spike-out of the weekend box), stop (beyond the running weekly extreme +0.05% buffer), TP (1.9R fixed), the refund rule, the Friday cap, fees 0.05%/side, slippage 0.02%/fill, and same-bar stop&TP ambiguity resolved on 1m (unresolvable = loss) are all imported verbatim from bt_weekly.py.
  • Retest unchanged. Still the 2-of-3 confluence entry {50-EMA wick-touch, return into the L1-vector range, HL/LH structure}. The abort-on-close-back-across-EMA guard is kept byte-identical and, as in bt_weekly, keys the “wrong side” off the fade direction (short → a close above the EMA aborts) — it never referenced the L1 candle’s own cross, so “no cross required” leaves the abort semantics untouched.
  • Loosened detector. find_vector_in_dir: first candle after the spike extreme with pv_class ≥ 200 (b) or ≥ 150 (c) whose direction matches the fade, using the same PVSRA columns (volume ≥ 2.0×/1.5× the prior-10 average) as the baseline detector. Same scan window as the baseline for a like-for-like test.
  • Mechanism verified separately. backtests/bt2_verify_mechanism.py re-derives every week's L1 under all three rules and confirms: superset invariant (loosened L1 ≤ baseline L1 position) holds in 266/266 weeks with 0 violations; 63/100 weeks common to baseline and loosened-200 pick the same L1 bar (identical trade), the rest fire a mean 26 bars earlier; and every baseline week lost under the loosening (123 full-set for 200%, 168 for 150%) aborts on a wrong-side close within ~3h of the early L1.
  • In-sample only. Week enumeration ends at 2025-01-01 (exclusive of 2025+); the locked 2025-26 OOS window is never enumerated in this file.
