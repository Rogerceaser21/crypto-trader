Wednesday Reversal Statistics

Igor’s two questions, answered from our own data: (1) How often do we get a reversal on a Wednesday? (2) When we do, which session does the reversing — US, UK, or Asia? This is a descriptive study only: event frequency and session attribution, no strategy and no trade simulation. Because a “reversal” can mean three different things, we pre-declared three definitions and computed all of them — no cherry-picking.

The two answers

Depends what you call a reversal — but when the week’s real turn lands on Wednesday, the US session prints it.

Q1 — how often: The week’s dominant extreme (its real turning point) lands on Wednesday only 13.2% of weeks (Thursday 16.3%, and Friday is actually the single most common at 21.4%). A crude “did Wednesday close flip the Mon–Tue drift” is a pure coin flip (51.3%, p = 0.67). But Annie’s actual M/W reversal structure confirms on the Wednesday trading day in 72.4% of weeks — as a chart event the midweek reversal is nearly ubiquitous.

Q2 — which session: When the week’s real turn is on a Wednesday, the US session prints it 61.7% of the time (UK 17.0%, Asia 10.6%) — exactly matching Annie’s “US = reversal.” At the finer M/W-structure grain the confirmation splits UK 40% / US 40% (Asia 20%). So: US first, UK second, Asia a distant third.

1 · Three definitions of “a reversal” — all computed, none cherry-picked

“Reversal” is ambiguous, so we declared three concrete meanings up front and report each separately. Every “Wednesday” below is the 5pm-NY trading day (Tue 17:00 NY → Wed 17:00 NY) — the project’s week anchor. Sessions are the project’s existing convention, not new ones.

D1 · Weekly-extreme pivot

The week’s dominant extreme — whichever of the week’s high or low is further from the week’s open in % — lands on Wednesday, and price then retraces ≥1% from it by the Friday-17:00-NY cutoff. This is the “the actual weekly turn happened on Wednesday” reading. Attribution = session of the extreme’s bar.

D2 · Daily direction flip

Wednesday’s 5pm-day direction (close vs open) is opposite to the combined Mon+Tue drift (Wed open vs Mon open). The crude “the day flipped the early-week trend” reading, benchmarked against a 50% coin flip with a binomial p-value. Attribution = session of the Wed high (down-flip) or low (up-flip).

D3 · M/W structure event (Annie’s actual mechanic)

The calibrated causal opposite-side M / W / Final-Damage detector on the 1H — reused verbatim from the F7/G3 engine (backtests/bt_midweek_counter.py, detector V2). Peak-2 is a k=3 fractal known only k bars later, so the confirmation bar b = peak2 + 3 is fully causal. We report the share of weeks whose first midweek confirmation (either side) fires on the Wednesday trading day, plus the session of the confirmation bar and, separately, the session of the peak-2 (the second-peak turn) bar. Gate: Wed 00:00 NY → Fri 08:00 NY, exactly as G3 defined “midweek.”

2 · D1 — Weekly-extreme pivot: the real turn lands on Wednesday 13% of weeks

Across all 355 weeks, we asked which weekday printed the week’s dominant extreme. Wednesday accounts for 13.2%; the ultimate turn is actually more likely on Thursday (16.3%) and most likely on Friday (21.4%) — weekly extremes cluster late. Every single Wed/Thu extreme retraced ≥1% by Friday (BTC’s weekly range dwarfs 1%), so all of these are genuine intraweek pivots, not runaway closes.

3 · D2 — Daily direction flip: a coin flip, no midweek signal

The bluntest reading — did Wednesday’s close reverse the Mon–Tue drift? — shows no Wednesday-specific tendency. It happens 51.3% of weeks, indistinguishable from a 50% coin flip (binomial two-sided p = 0.67). Thursday is the same (50.7%, p = 0.83). Raw daily direction carries no “midweek reversal” edge; the structure (D3) is where the pattern lives.

4 · D3 — M/W structure (Annie’s mechanic): confirms Wednesday in 72% of weeks

Using the exact calibrated F7/G3 detector, some midweek M/W reversal fires in almost every week (347/355 = 97.7%), and its first confirmation lands specifically on the Wednesday trading day in 257/355 = 72.4% of weeks (Thursday 22.0%, Friday 3.4%). As a chart event the midweek reversal is real and common — which is what Annie teaches. Its session is where it gets interesting.

5 · So which session “does the reversing”?

Three lenses, one consistent lead answer: US first, UK second, Asia third. The only place the ordering shifts is the fine-grained M/W internals, where UK draws level with (or ahead of) US.

If you zoom into the M/W micro-structure, the turn itself often forms late in the UK session and the pattern confirms across UK/US. Asia is consistently the least likely reversal session at every grain.

6 · Important caveat — a common event is not a tradeable edge

This page counts events; it does not claim a trade. The companion study The Midweek Counter Trade (G3) already tested whether you can counter-trade this midweek peak, across 6 declared cells and 6 years: every cell lost money, with reliably negative t-stats (−2.1 to −4.2). After the signal fires, price on average keeps moving against the pattern — countering the midweek peak means fighting the dominant drift. So the Wednesday reversal is a real, frequent structural event (this page) that is not tradeable as a counter (G3). Both facts hold at once; nothing here revisits or contradicts that ruling.

7 · Method & conventions

Time anchors (reused, not invented)

  • Week = Sunday 17:00 NY → next Sunday 17:00 NY (anchors.week_open).
  • Trading day = 5pm-NY day; a bar at/after 17:00 NY belongs to the next day. “Wednesday” = Tue 17:00 → Wed 17:00 NY (anchors.trading_day_name).
  • Sessions (NY local): Asia 19:00–02:00 · UK 02:00–08:00 · US 08:00–17:00 · Dead-Zone 17:00–19:00 (anchors.session_of). Igor asked Asia/UK/US; the project convention adds a 2h post-US Dead-Zone, reported honestly.

Rigor

  • Causality: extremes and attributions are computed after the week/day closes — correct for a descriptive study (we describe what happened, not trade it). The D3 detector is itself fully causal (confirmation known k fractal-bars later).
  • D3 reuse: the M/W/FD scan is bt_midweek_counter.first_midweek called verbatim; over the in-sample window it reproduces the G3 ledger (Wed 199→203, Thu 58→60 — the tiny surplus is confirmations G3 later dropped on stop-geometry, which don’t change the event count).
  • Deterministic: the whole pipeline was run twice; all four results CSVs are byte-identical (md5 below).
  • Data: full 2019-09 → 2026-07-11, including 2025–26 — descriptive only, so the OOS backtest lockout is left untouched.
