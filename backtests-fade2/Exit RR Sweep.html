Best Exit for the Monday Fade — R:R Sweep

Same frozen entries every time (the validated 91 Monday-spike fades, +0.382R, t=2.98). We hold entry / stop / refund / Friday-cap fixed and vary ONLY the exit target: five fixed R:R levels (1.9R baseline, 2.5R, 3R, 4R, 5R) plus a 50%-partial scale-out. Judged head-to-head on the identical trades. Causal, in-sample 2019–2024, realistic fees and slippage. The locked 2025–26 block is untouched.

⚠ A BETTER TARGET EXISTS — CONFIRM ON OOS FIRSTThe 1.9R baseline is not the best fixed exit. Widening the target to 2.5R lifts expectancy +0.382R → +0.506R (+32%) and total +34.8R → +46.0R, and it is the only variant that clears the pre-registered paired-t ≥ 2.5 bar (t=2.56). 3.0R books the most money (+0.541R, +49R) but at paired t=2.22, just under the bar. The catch: the win is marginal (2.56 barely clears 2.5; after correcting for the 5 challengers p ≈ 0.06), drawdown grows 4.6R → 6.1R, and it is strong on the primary and mid sets but flat on the smallest. Promote 2.5R (watch 3.0R) to the locked out-of-sample run as the challenger; do not switch live until it survives. 4R, 5R and the 50%-partial do not beat baseline.

7 · Honesty box

  • Pre-registered grid, all reported. All six exits (1.9R / 2.5R / 3R / 4R / 5R / 50%-partial) were declared before running; every one is in the table; none was added or dropped after seeing results.
  • Multiple-testing bar. 5 challengers vs the 1.9R baseline → the pre-set bar is paired t ≥ 2.5. Only 2.5R clears it (t=2.56, uncorrected p=0.012). Bonferroni across the 5 tests puts it at p ≈ 0.06 — it clears the declared bar but is borderline after correction, not a slam dunk.
  • Higher average is not enough. 3.0R has the highest expectancy (+0.541R) and total (+49R) of any variant, yet its paired t is 2.22 — below the bar. It is reported as promising, not proven.
  • Return AND drawdown, together. The wider target's extra return is bought with a deeper drawdown (4.6R→6.1R at 2.5–3R). The verdict weighs both.
  • Same-bar ambiguity = loss. When a 15m bar touches both stop and target, the minute data decides and a same-minute tie is scored a loss — the conservative choice, applied identically to every variant and every target.
  • Partial is a conservative single-management model. The 50%-partial books exactly half at 1.9R, moves the remainder to breakeven, and runs it to the Friday cap with no second target — each leg is half-size (half R). Real discretionary scaling can only be tested as declared, not tuned.
  • Determinism. The full sweep was run twice; the per-trade output CSVs are md5-identical across runs.

8 · What to change, if anything

Take the 2.5R target to the locked out-of-sample run as the challenger. On the validated 91 it is the single exit that clears the pre-registered paired-t bar, lifting expectancy +32% and total return +32%, and the same lift shows up on the loosened Set B. That is enough to test it OOS — not enough to switch live today, because the clearance is marginal (2.56 vs 2.5) and borderline after multiple-comparisons correction.

Watch 3.0R alongside it. It books the most money of any variant (+0.541R, +49R) at the same 6.1R drawdown as 2.5R, and fits the mechanism (the Monday reversion tends to overshoot toward the 2.5–3R zone). It just falls short of the significance bar here. If both survive OOS, prefer whichever holds its edge; if only one does, that is the new target.

Do not widen to 4R or 5R. Expectancy falls back and drawdown blows out to 8.9R — the fade simply does not run that far inside the trading week; over half the trades time out on Friday.

Skip the 50%-partial. It is statistically tied with the baseline (paired t=0.63) and carries a deeper drawdown (6.2R vs 4.6R). Booking half at 1.9R throws away the very overshoot that the wider target is there to capture.

Keep the baseline live until OOS confirms. The 1.9R exit remains the validated default; 2.5R is the pending challenger, not yet the live rule.
