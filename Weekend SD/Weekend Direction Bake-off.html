How the test works

Each rule makes one call per weekend — long or short — then holds with no stop to the weekend end. No stop is deliberate: a stop would mix exit skill into the score; holding the whole leg isolates direction skill alone. We then compare each rule to the always-long baseline entered at the same moment, weekend by weekend (a paired difference). The headline number is that mean excess vs long and its paired t-stat: positive and significant means the rule genuinely adds direction skill; anything else means it does not.

Blue dots are the four entry moments; green dashed line is the weekend end (base). Each rule picks long or short at an entry and holds to the exit. The score is how much that beats always-long entered at the very same dot.

Exit note: E1–E3 enter before the weekend base (Mon 02:00 Dubai) and exit there — the weekend move. E4 (Sun 21:00 NY) lands 3–4h after that base, so it cannot exit there; it exits at the Monday-window close (Tue 00:00 Dubai), the same leg Phase 4a called the “Monday remainder.” Within every entry column the rule and its long-only baseline share the identical entry and exit, so the comparison is apples-to-apples. This was fixed before any result was computed.

So there are no “no-pattern” skips; the detector always has an opinion (99 M, 140 W, 18 FDM, 20 FDW).
  • Recent-drift seasonality (D2) needs 26 completed weekends of history, so its first 26 weekends are warm-up (no trade): N = 251, not 277.
  • All other rules trade all 277 weekends.

Honesty box.
  • Multiple-comparison math. With 21 cells, roughly one |t| ≥ 2 is expected by pure luck. On the key paired-excess statistic, at the pre-registered entry times, zero cells even reach |t| ≥ 2 — the most extreme is D5×E3 at t = −1.91, and that is a rule that hurts you.
  • The one that flickers over the line (D6×E4 at −1h, t = +2.06) is exactly the kind of lone |t| ≥ 2 chance produces, and it fails the ±1h robustness check outright.
  • Why they lose. Almost every rule is short-biased (it calls a lot of shorts). 2019–2024 was net-bullish, so those shorts bled against a drifting-up tape — which is why long-only is hard to beat and the rules underperform it.
  • Pre-registration honoured. The grid was frozen up front; the E4 entry was deliberately set to the SUN-POST close (21:00 NY), not its open, for causality — decided before any number was seen. Nothing was added after results.
  • This is the expected outcome: Phase 4a already found only marginal direction signals (its best, the same late-Sunday momentum, cleared |t| ≥ 2 for a single test but not the multiple-comparison bar). Phase 4b confirms it as a full bake-off: no rule earns its keep on direction.
What this means for the strategy. Trade the weekend leg long-only (or don't trade direction at all) — there is no mechanical filter here that improves on it. If the weekend leg is to earn anything, the edge has to come from somewhere other than picking up/down: sizing, the mean-reversion fade already studied, or an entirely different leg. Do not deploy any of D2–D6 as a direction filter.
