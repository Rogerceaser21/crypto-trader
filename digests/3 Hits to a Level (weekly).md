# Digest: 3 Hits to a Level (weekly)

## Summary
Presents "three hits to a level" as a reversal trigger that can **replace** the M/W at the weekly high/low: three failed tests of the HOW/LOW while price is at level 3 → reversal imminent; a fourth hit → continuation likely. Adds strict counting rules (hits must be separated by a move-away, max two per session, not all three in one session) and a directional tiebreaker read from market structure after the third hit.

## Exact rules
- [00:00] Best setups of the method: M at HOW / W at LOW after three levels complete — but other reversal signals can **replace** the M/W.
- [00:00]–[00:30] If the MM tests a weekly high or low **three times without breaking it**, a reversal is likely imminent. **Condition: price must be at level 3.**
- [00:30] If they hit the level a **fourth** time, continuation is likely.
- [00:30]–[01:00] Additionally assess, after each rejection/move-away, whether market structure changes — this distinguishes trend change from continuation. This zone is "not technically a board meeting," but it's where the MM evaluates their position/liquidity and decides push-through vs fall-down.
- [01:00]–[01:31] **Counting rules:** (a) the hits need to be **in different sessions**, not one after the other; (b) after a hit, price must **move away and then come back** for the next touch to count; (c) **up to two hits max in one session**; (d) all three hits in the same session does NOT qualify.
- [01:31]–[02:01] Qualifying example: at level-3 rise, price hits HOW and moves away in Asia → second failed attempt toward the end of Asia → third attempt in UK = qualifies as three attempts.
- [02:01] **Directional tiebreaker after the third hit:** if price has been making higher lows on each rejection and, after the third attempt, **yet another higher low** forms → assume break to the UPSIDE next. If a **lower high** forms → assume break to the DOWNSIDE next.
- [02:01]–[02:31] Confluence boosters: a news event lining up, or the **midweek reversal (MWR) being due**.
- [02:31] Besides this M/W-replacement trigger there is one more: the **200 EMA rejection trade** — named "the second setup in the weekly cycle," covered in the EMA lesson.
- [02:31]–[03:31] Chart example: two hits at the high **within the US session** (valid because after the first hit — a wick — price moved away, then came back up for the second hit, then moved away again); the third hit came in a **new session (Asia)**; then a **lower high** formed (red arrow) = the hint to take a **short**.

## Pattern definition attempt
**Three-hits reversal (short at HOW; long at LOW mirror):**
- Level: the current weekly high (or low).
- Hit = a distinct test of the level: price touches/approaches the level, then **moves away**, then returns. Consecutive candles sitting at the level = one hit, not several.
- Constraints: total 3 hits, no break of the level; ≤2 hits per session; the 3 hits span ≥2 sessions; price must be at level 3 of the weekly count.
- Trigger: after hit #3's rejection, a **lower high** forms (for the short at HOW) → enter short. If instead **another higher low** forms → stand down / expect upside break (and a 4th hit implies continuation).
- Extra confluence: MWR due (Wednesday) or aligned news event.
- Invalidation: level breaks on any hit; a 4th hit occurs; higher low keeps printing after hit #3 (at HOW).
- Undefined: touch tolerance to the level (wick within X? exact tick?), minimum "move away" distance/time, the timeframe on which hits and the lower high are read (probably 1H per the weekly-model lessons — never stated), and entry/stop/TP mechanics (not covered here at all).
