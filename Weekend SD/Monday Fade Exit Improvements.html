6 · Honesty box

  • Pre-registered, all reported. All 8 exits were declared before running; every one is in the table above; no exit was added or dropped after seeing results.
  • Multiple-testing bar. 7 challengers vs baseline → a real improvement needs paired t ≥ 2.5, not just a higher average. 0 clear it.
  • Return AND drawdown, together. A variant with equal return but half the drawdown is a real finding and is called out; a variant with a higher average but a worse or statistically-tied paired t is not.
  • Same-bar ambiguity = loss. When a 15m bar touches both stop and target, the minute data decides and a same-minute tie is scored as a loss — the conservative choice, applied to every variant.
  • Variant 7 caveat. A literal "Monday-window close" is degenerate here: 65/91 entries land after Tue-00:00 Dubai because the mechanical L1-break + retest lags into Tue/Wed. "Flat overnight" is therefore read as "close by the end of the Dubai day of entry" — the honest non-degenerate form.
  • Conservative floor. As in the flagship, this is a single-unit model; real partial-scaling management (Variant 4 is the closest proxy) can only be tested as declared, not tuned.

7 · What to change in live trading, if anything

Keep the baseline exit (fixed 1.9R target) for now. No alternative exit produced a statistically real improvement on the identical entries — the best challenger, Target the opposite-side average level, is +0.491R vs +0.382R but at paired t=1.22, short of the 2.5 bar. Switching live today would be trading noise.

One thing worth watching (not yet trading): aiming past the base to the opposite-side average level (Variant 3) numerically dominates baseline — higher win rate (60%), higher average (+0.491R), higher total (+45R), same 4.6R drawdown — which fits the mechanism (the Monday reversion tends to overshoot the weekend base, not stop at it). It just is not significant on the paired test here. If it survives the locked out-of-sample block, it becomes a live change; until then, do not.

Optional, risk-only: if a smoother equity curve matters more than the last basis point of return, ATR trailing stop after 1R (no fixed target) keeps expectancy at +0.343R (vs +0.382R baseline) with a higher win rate (67%) and a modestly lower drawdown (4.6R→4.0R). That is a variance preference, not an edge improvement — the baseline drawdown is already shallow.

Drop the refund rule from the mental checklist for this setup — it never fires (the stop pre-empts it), so it is cognitive load with zero effect (Variant 8 confirms).

Do not shorten the hold. The base-magnet and opposite-mean targets and the overnight-flat rule all give away the multi-day mean reversion that makes this fade pay; the average winning fade needs ~43 hours to work.
