Annie Trading · Fade 1 (Weekly FMWB Monday fade) · live-executability audit

Live Execution Reality Check

The validated Monday fade earns +0.382R / trade across 91 trades — but that ledger quietly assumes you knew in hindsight which early-week spike was THE spike (the single furthest poke out of the weekend box over all of Sun/Mon/early-Tue). In real time you never have that. This page runs the exact same fade with real fills in the two ways you actually could have traded it live — and asks the only question that matters: what would you actually have made?

Verdict · first

Only streaming keeps the edge. Lock-at-close destroys it.

Committing to the running extreme in real time (Streaming — the mode the shipped Pine indicator actually uses) preserves a real, significant edge: +0.257R/trade over 167 trades, t = 2.46, and in fact a higher total R than the anchor because it trades more often. Waiting for the Tuesday early-window close (Lock-at-close) collapses the edge to +0.041R/trade (t = 0.36, indistinguishable from zero): 71 of 91 fills arrive a median 22 hours late at worse prices, surrendering ~89% of the total R.

You cannot cherry-pick the good entries in real time — picking only the 75 that reproduce the validated ledger requires exactly the hindsight you don't have. Fade 1 remains a positive, statistically-real edge live — just a thinner and choppier one than the validated ledger shows, and worthless if execution has to wait for the early window to close.

1 · Why the validated ledger isn’t the live ledger

The validated detector scores the whole early window (Sun 17:00 → Tue 17:00 NY) at once, then fades the single furthest excursion out of the weekend box. That is a legitimate design measurement, but it is a look-back: on Monday morning you don’t yet know that the extreme you’re watching won’t be beaten by a bigger one on Tuesday. Two honest ways to actually push the button:

A  Streaming

Commit to the running extreme the instant a retest chain fires. Reproduces the validated entries and takes phantom trades a later, bigger spike would have retroactively invalidated. Stop is set from the running weekly extreme at the entry bar; if a new extreme prints afterwards the stop does not move — a live stop is fixed at entry. Entry logic reused verbatim from f10_streaming_mirror.detect_week_streaming (first chain per week).

B  Lock-at-close

No phantoms — you wait for the early window to close (Tue 17:00 NY) so the winning spike is knowable, then take one trade for the week. Fill = the next 15m open after the signal is knowable: if the chain completed after the lock you get the true bar (= validated); if it completed before the lock you could not have acted, so you fill at the lock bar — often a day late. Stop/TP are re-derived from the actual fill (a live trader sizes off the fill they really got).

Anchor (correctness gate). Before believing either live mode, the same harness re-runs the validated detection on ideal fills at the true entry bar and must reproduce the validated 91 / +0.382R ledger (f3_exits_ledger_a.csv, column R__fix_1.9) to the last decimal. It does — see the proof band.

4 · Streaming’s hidden tax: phantom trades

Split the 167 streaming trades by whether they reproduce a validated entry (week + entry within ±2 bars) or are phantoms — running-extreme fades the hindsight engine would never have taken. 55% are phantoms. They are not disasters, but they are close to dead money, and they are why streaming’s per-trade edge is a third thinner than the ledger and its win-rate and profit-factor sag.

The catch you can’t engineer around. The 75 “good” streaming trades are excellent (+0.486R, PF 2.49), but they are only knowable as good after the week resolves. In real time the phantom and the keeper look identical at entry — both are the running extreme’s retest. You take all 167 or you don’t stream. The phantom drag (+0.070R over 92 trades) is the honest price of live entry timing.

5 · Lock-at-close: the delay cost, itemised

Lock-at-close has no phantoms — it takes the same 91 validated weeks. Its problem is purely timing: the fade’s edge lives in entering as the retest confirms, and most confirmations happen on Monday, long before the Tuesday-17:00 lock lets a lock-trader act.

Fill timing (1.9R)countWin%exp R vs anchornote
True bar (chain completes after the lock) 20 / 91——identical Reproduces the anchor trade exactly (max |ΔR| = 0).
Late fill (chain completes before the lock) 71 / 9140.8%−0.003gutted Filled at the lock bar, median 22.0h late (max 42.8h), at lock-bar prices.

Where the R went

  • Paired over the same 91 weeks: anchor +34.76R → lock +3.73R = −31.03R handed to delay.
  • The 71 late fills alone average −0.003R — the whole surviving edge is carried by the 20 true-bar fills.
  • By the time the lock lets you in, price has usually already travelled toward TP, so you enter worse and get stopped or Friday-closed instead.
