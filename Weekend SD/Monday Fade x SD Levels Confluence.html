Monday Fade × Weekend SD Levels — a confluence filter

Igor's question: does the validated Monday fade get better when the M/W spike peak lands inside the weekend Standard-Deviation bands (History, 3-Month, 4-Week)? No new trades — same fade, filtered.

Verdict · Suggestive

🟡 The History-SD line is a real, usable filter; the other two are not. Keeping only the Mondays whose peak reached at least the History-SD mean lifts the fade from +0.382R to +0.502R (55 trades, 60% win, t = 3.3). But it is a soft tilt, not a proven money-machine.

The improvement clears the pre-registered "believe it" bar on its own (t ≥ 3 with N ≥ 40), yet the head-to-head test of kept vs dropped Mondays is only a two-sample t ≈ 1.1 — so the extra +0.12R is directionally right but not statistically clean. The 3-Month SD line actually points the other way, and the exact "confluence zone" almost never triggers (3 trades). Trade it as a preference, not a gate.

What was tested

Same validated fade, sorted by where the peak landed

The validated Monday fade is unchanged: an early-week spike-out of the weekend box, a 200% PVSRA vector body-closing through the 50 EMA, a 2-of-3 retest, then fade back with a fixed 1.9R target and the stop beyond the spike. We took its frozen trade list and, for every trade, measured how far the formation peak stretched from the weekend base, then asked which of that weekend's SD bands the peak sat in.

Anchor reproduction (proof the trade list is the real one)

Monday fade = +0.382R over 91 trades (t = 2.98) · all-week = +0.099R over 223 trades (t = 1.23). These match the validated backtest exactly — the filter study is built on the identical ledger, R values read verbatim.

The three SD sources are exactly the lines the dynamic indicator draws, computed causally (each weekend uses only earlier weekends — zero look-ahead):

  • History SD — expanding, all weekends so far (the slow, stable anchor).
  • 3-Month SD — trailing 13 weekends.
  • 4-Week SD — trailing 4 weekends (fast but noisy).

Note: on TradingView the History line is a fixed constant (the chart can't hold 5 years of 1-minute bars); here it is the strictly-causal expanding version, which is the honest thing to test. The 3-Month and 4-Week lines are identical to the indicator's live sets.

Does the filter actually add anything? (the honest head-to-head)

The right test is not "is the kept group profitable" (it inherits the already-significant Monday edge) but "are the kept Mondays better than the dropped ones". Two-sample Welch t of reached-mean minus below-mean R, Monday:

Read this honestly

The History-SD filter raises expectancy by +0.12R and lifts win-rate to 60%, but the two-sample t of +1.1 means we cannot claim the kept Mondays are statistically distinct from the dropped ones. It is a genuine tilt in the right direction with weak separation — use it to size up stretched Mondays, not to skip the rest.

Only 3 Monday trades ever landed inside an exact agreement zone — far too few to conclude anything (they happened to average +0.85R, but that is noise). The tight 0.25-point agreement band is simply too narrow for a fast-moving peak to sit in. Reaching beyond p95 (a true statistical extreme) also gave no edge over staying within it (History: +0.51R vs +0.37R, both thin/noisy). The usable signal is the plain mean line, not the exotic zones.

Honesty box

  • 91-trade Monday bins are thin. Splitting them into 3–4 groups leaves cells that swing on a handful of trades — every greyed (N<20) row is illustration, not evidence.
  • We ran roughly 30 comparisons. By chance alone 1–2 of them should show |t| ≥ 2. The History-SD reached-mean cell (t = 3.3) is the one result that clears the stricter pre-registered bar (t ≥ 3, N ≥ 40) — but its incremental value over the plain fade is only a two-sample t ≈ 1.1.
  • The three sources disagree: History helps, 4-Week is neutral, 3-Month inverts. A robust filter would show the same sign across all three. It does not. That is a caution flag, not a green light.
  • This is in-sample (2019–2024). No out-of-sample confirmation of the filter has been run — the validated fade itself held out-of-sample, but the SD filter has not been tested there.
  • Nothing here changes the entries or exits. It is a preference layer on an already-validated trade.
Practical use

How to use this on the chart

With the Weekend + Monday SD Levels (V3 dynamic) indicator on your BTC chart, when a Monday fade sets up:

  1. Find the grey History-SD "UP mean" / "DOWN mean" line for this weekend (the slow anchor line, plotted from the weekend base). This is the one that carried the signal.
  2. Did the spike peak reach or exceed that mean line before your fade trigger? If yes, this is a "stretched" Monday — historically the stronger half (60% win, +0.50R vs +0.38R). Take it with normal-to-slightly-larger conviction.
  3. If the peak never reached the mean line, it is a "shallow" Monday (+0.19R historically). Still positive, but the softer half — smaller size, or stand aside if you are being selective.
  4. Ignore the 3-Month and 4-Week lines for this filter — they did not help (3-Month actively misled). Use them only as visual context / the confluence shading.
  5. Don't wait for the confluence box or a p95 tag — those almost never contain the peak intraday and add nothing.
  6. Outside Monday, lean on the daily trend instead: only fade in the direction of the daily 4-week momentum — against-trend fades on Tue–Wed were flat.

Bottom line: the History-SD mean line is a reasonable "is this Monday stretched enough" checkbox that nudges the odds; it is not a hard gate and not proven, so keep position sizes where the validated fade already put them.
