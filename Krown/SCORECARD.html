The bottom line

After the full campaign

All sixteen of Krown's own strategies are dead or blocked. Twelve were tested and lost money after costs; four can never be tested because they rest on proprietary indicators we cannot rebuild from price.

Six further builds were then constructed specifically to rescue those ideas — lifting them to slower timeframes, swapping in the real stop, mechanising the selection, adding the divergence filter. Every one failed to beat a plain trend benchmark.

The single positive edge the whole program produced is that benchmark itself: a daily / 4-hour trend follower built from the literature-standard momentum and breakout rules — not one of Krown's scalp recipes. His ideas were measured against it, and lost. It is proven in-sample only; the 2025–26 out-of-sample window has never been touched.

Read it in one line: 1 winner, 22 dead or blocked — and the winner is the control, not the course. The edge is real but not yet confirmed: it still owes an independent re-run and the one-shot out-of-sample test before a single dollar, even paper, rides on it.

Read it in one line: the trend rule gives up raw return (Bitcoin's five-year bull was enormous) but earns the same return-per-risk while side-stepping the 2022 crash. A −77% hold-drawdown ends an account; −15% is survivable. That trade-off — not the headline number — is the whole point of a trend product.

The finding that killed the scalps

Why trading faster quietly destroys the edge

Every round-trip costs about 0.14% in fees and slippage. That fixed toll is the wall every idea must clear — and almost all of Krown's are scalps.

The same trend signal, run at three speeds. Slow it down and it clears the cost wall with room to spare; speed it up and the profit per trade shrinks until it drowns beneath the fees:

26×
Daily: 3.70% gross per trade — 26 times the cost. Edge survives.
8×
4-hour: 1.18% gross per trade — well above cost. Edge survives.
0.06×
1-hour: 0.01% gross per trade — below cost. Net turns negative. Edge dies.
Why this matters for Krown: nearly every one of his sixteen strategies lives on the 5m/15m timeframe — the one speed where the cost toll eats the edge alive. This single picture is why the scalps were always fighting a losing battle.

Daily trend follower — the benchmark & the one winner

✓ WINNER · in-sample only
avg win +16.62%avg loss −2.69%payoff 6.17×$10k → $11,082
trend-followingdailyno Krown indicators

The idea. Strip trend-following back to what the academic literature proves survives fees: on the daily chart go long when the last few weeks are up, ride it with a wide ATR (Chandelier) trailing stop, and shrink size when the market is choppy. Deliberately generic — the neutral yardstick every Krown rebuild has to beat. This is not a Krown recipe.

The result. It works, risk-adjusted. Center cell (canonical 4-week TSMOM, MA200-gated): Sharpe 0.93 / MAR 0.96, +104.7% total return at a −15% worst drawdown. Best cell: Sharpe 1.31 / MAR 2.06 (t 2.36). Buy-and-hold returned far more (+799%) but at a −77% drawdown — the kind that ends an account. 18 of 384 cells clear expectancy>0 AND t>2.

Why it matters: because a proven trend edge does show up on our own Bitcoin candles, the testing engine is sound. Any Krown strategy that can't beat this plain benchmark is adding complexity for nothing — and none of them did.

Where the knowledge stands

Settled vs still open

✓ Settled by this campaign

  • Scalps don't clear the cost floor. At 1-hour and below, gross profit per trade (≈0.01%) sits below the 0.14% round-trip cost. Only the 1D and 4H timeframes survive — and Krown's setups are almost all 5m/15m.
  • Stop geometry is not the edge. The real LuxAlgo stop (C5) lifts win-rate +8.5 points and fixes the cost-band pathology, but expectancy stays negative. It explains part of the 84%-WR gap, not the missing edge.
  • Divergence doesn't help. The RSI-divergence “sharpener” (C7) beats nothing after multiple-testing correction: 0/144 cells survive FDR.
  • The dip-buy family is closed. Even the fairest rebuild — trend-gated, calibrated, cost-multiple target (C4) — reaches 0/432 significant cells.

⚠ Still open

  • The winner is in-sample only. The daily / 4H trend edge (C2 → T4) is proven on 2019–2024 history only. It still owes two gates before it is real: an independent Jesse cross-check and the one-shot 2025–26 out-of-sample run. Neither has been done yet.
  • The out-of-sample window is untouched. It is spent exactly once, ever, and only on a survivor that has already passed the cross-check. Nothing has burned it.
  • S6–S9 are blocked, not dead. The Krown Stochastic and JewelP base oscillators are proprietary. If the Pine source ever surfaces, four strategies become testable — until then they are parked with a reason.

Causal only. Every signal is computed on the close of bar t and filled at the next bar's open — no look-ahead, ever. Ambiguous fills are losses: when a stop and a target could both hit in one bar, the stop wins. Grids are declared before results and frozen, so nothing is fitted after the fact.

Exchange caveat. Our candles are Binance perpetual; Krown's on-screen sheets are Bybit.
