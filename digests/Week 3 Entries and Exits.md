# Week 3 Entries and Exits — Digest

## Summary
Third worked-example week: the weekend trap issued as a W INSIDE the weekend box (no spike/stop-hunt), a textbook FMWB trade, the "perfect" M at the Asia→UK changeover at the high of the week, and the week's most important management rule — a vertical extended move followed by an extended board meeting means "the damage is done" (the 3 levels may have completed on the 15m), so take profit before the news event rather than holding into it.

## Exact rules

### Weekend trap variants
- [00:00–00:30] If there is no false breakout / spike out of the weekend box, look for W and M formations inside the weekend box — "it's another way to issue the trap". Rule: "Look for Ws and Ms if there is no spike out of the weekend box."
- [00:30] Here: W in the weekend → false move (FMWB) to the upside; then the M formed "over Asia into UK, which is the perfect spot for the M".

### FMWB trade (W)
- [01:00–01:30] The W broke the 50 EMA with volume, came back to retest it, then ran three levels back to the high of the week = "perfect target". Result 3.9:1.
- [01:30] She "probably would not take a conservative entry on a false move week beginning trade".
- [01:30–02:01] You may trade false moves (FMWB and false-move Monday — they're really daily-setup trades), but you must KNOW you're in a false move: "as long as you know that, you don't try to hold past the three levels. And that's how you end up not getting trapped."

### Real trend (M at Asia→UK at the HOW)
- [02:01–02:31] Spike into the high of the week, price came back to repeat it, "could not get back into those wick areas — that's where the traders were trapped". Aggressive entry = any of the candles that rejected the trapped-trader wick zone.
- [02:31] Level 1 broke the 50 EMA "just with volume — it kind of only just passes because it did wick back up. So it's not ideal, but it worked out anyway."
- [02:31–03:01] Location quality rule: "an M or W at Asia over UK [changeover] at the high of the week — there's no better entry than this."
- [03:01–03:32] Conservative entry: price hovered around the 50 EMA a long time but that was NOT a good R:R entry; the correct wait was for price to come back to the unrecovered vector ("that big red one from the drop"). Stop above the wick of the stop hunt into that vector; target = a previous vector. Results: aggressive 6.7:1, conservative 2.7:1 (aggressive better this week).

### MWR trade (W into news) + the "damage is done" rule
- [03:32–04:03] MWR: one spike low, then a second spike to the same area = "your confirmation that that is a W. Take your entry there." Wicks were large → necessarily a fairly large stop. Lined up with the news event.
- [04:03–04:33] Trading MWR on the 1H you'd normally look for 3 levels of rise on the 1H — but this week it gave only one 1H level because the news event "changed the rules and ended the week".
- [04:33–05:03] KEY RULE (her words): "When price has a big extended move, like basically vertical, and then it goes into an extended board meeting, the damage is done. It means they don't really need to run any more levels." Consequence here: the 3 levels ran on the 15m instead of the 1H, straight into the area of interest.
- [05:03–05:34] Pre-news management: if you can count 3 levels on the 15m and price only goes sideways into a scheduled news event (Core PCE), do NOT hold through the news — either protect with the stop or take profit. "They're accumulating contracts in that board meeting right before the news for a reason." The news produced a double-sided stop hunt that following the rule would have avoided.
- [05:34–06:04] The news was at the end of Friday UK, "where you should close your trades anyway" — weekly trades are closed by end of Friday UK session.

## New jargon (not in glossary)
- **"Damage is done" rule** — vertical move + extended board meeting ⇒ no further levels owed; count may complete on a lower timeframe (15m instead of 1H).

- **Accumulating contracts** — MM building position inside the pre-news board meeting.
