Decoupled Stop / TP Test

F11 (Stop Buffer Research) found that a wider stop rescues about half the baseline stop-outs — the genuine MM wick-raids — worth roughly +12R on that subset, but the rescue evaporated because the fixed-R target scaled with the wider stop and moved out of reach. This is the fix that finding pointed at: back the hard stop off for raid protection, but keep the take-profit LEVEL nailed to the original tight risk unit. Same 91 frozen entries, in-sample 2019→2024. The only question: once the target stays put, does the raid rescue finally pay?

No. Decoupling does not rescue the fade. answer: NO Holding the TP on the baseline geometry, every one of the 6 cells finishes below its matched baseline — full-book R falls in all six. The raid rescue is real (the wider stop does save the wick-hunts, +10.4R on the 15-trade raid subset at 0.75%), but sizing honestly on the wider stop shrinks the reward per winner across the whole book, and that cost on the other 76 trades (−15.2R) more than eats the rescue.

  • Best (least-bad) cell: 0.75% stop + 1.9R — expectancy 0.382→0.330R, stop-outs 29→13, drawdown 4.6→3.7R, but total R 34.8→30.0 and paired t vs the 0.05% baseline is −0.89. It is a calmer equity curve that gives up ~5R of edge — comfort, not profit.
  • Highest raw expectancy of the six: 0.75% stop + 2.5R at 0.415R — still short of its own 2.5R baseline (0.506R, paired t −1.44). No cell beats the target it is measured against.
  • Two cells lose significantly against the 2.5R baseline: 0.50%+1.9R (paired t −3.03) and 0.50%+2.5R (−2.91). None wins significantly.
  • The mechanism, isolated: F11 blamed the moving target. F15 removes it — and the strategy still loses R. So the price of raid protection is structural: a wider risk unit shrinks every winner’s reward-to-realized-risk ratio. The tight baseline stop is genuinely optimal here; the raids are the cost of the edge.

Anchor — the two baselines are the frozen, validated ledger itself (f3_exits_ledger_a.csv): 0.05%+1.9R mean +0.381994R (tot 34.76), 0.05%+2.5R mean +0.505594R (tot 46.01). Wick recovered two ways agrees to 7.3×10−12 on all 91. Both determinism runs md5-identical.

1 · What “decouple” changes

The baseline fade sizes and targets off one distance: the tight stop 5bp beyond the wick. F11 varied that distance and let both the size and the fixed-R target move with it. F15 splits them: the stop is a wide raid guard, but the target level is frozen to the baseline risk unit stop_dist_0 = |entry − stop0.05%|.

Hard stop (widened)

wick × (1 ± buf)
buf ∈ {0.5%, 0.75%, 1.0%}. Dodges the intrabar wick-raid.
→

Size (on the REAL stop)

$100 / wide_stop_dist
Honest: a stop-out is −1R on the wide stop.
→

Target LEVEL (frozen)

entry ± k × stop_dist0
k ∈ {1.9, 2.5}. Anchored to the baseline geometry — does not move with the stop.
The honest price, stated up front. Because size is on the wider stop, a TP hit now pays only TP_dist / wide_stop_dist = k × (stop_dist0 / wide_stop_dist) R — strictly less than k. The reward-to-realized-risk ratio shrinks with every basis point of extra stop. That shrink, spread across all 91 trades, is what the rescue has to beat. It doesn’t.

The trap is in the exit mix. Widen to 0.75%+1.9R and you get more TP hits than the baseline (35 vs 32) and less than half the stop-outs (13 vs 29) — a nicer-looking book by every count except the one that matters. Total R still falls 34.8→30.0, because every one of those 35 winners is paid at a shrunk R, and 11 raid survivors settle for near-breakeven refund exits instead of a clean target. More winners, less money.

The rescue is real; it just isn’t enough. At 0.75%+1.9R the wider stop wins back +10.40R on the raid subset — 10 of the 15 survive, 3 reach the kept target, 3 turn net-positive. That is exactly the effect your MM-raid instinct predicted. But sizing every trade on the wider stop costs −15.15R on the other 76, so the full book still drops −4.75R. At 1.0%, more raids survive (still 3 to TP) yet the cost grows faster (−17.70R) and the book falls further. There is no buffer where the rescue outruns the shrink.
Why so many raid survivors don’t reach TP. Of 10 survivors at 0.75%+1.9R only 3 hit the target; 5 exit on the raw-wick refund and 2 on Friday. Backing the stop off keeps them alive through the intrabar poke, but the refund-on-close-through-wick rule still ejects them the moment the bar closes the wrong side of the wick — and a target sized on the tight baseline unit is often still a long way up. Protection buys survival, not a win.

4 · The F11 hypothesis, settled

F11’s closing note read: “the fix implied by the data is to keep the TP on the original tight risk unit while backing the hard stop off ~0.5–0.75% so intrabar raids miss it — a clean, declared next test.” This is that test, and it converts an open question into a closed one.

The one thing decoupling buys cleanly is a smoother ride: at 0.75% the max drawdown drops from 4.6R to 3.7R and stop-outs more than halve. If you value equity-curve calm over expectancy you could run 0.75%+1.9R and knowingly donate ~5R of edge over the sample — but that is a comfort trade, and the paired-t (−0.89) says the sample can’t even prove it’s worse, let alone better. On the evidence, leave the stop tight.

5 · Method & reproducibility

  • Entries frozen. The 91 Monday-fade entries come straight from backtests/f3_exits_ledger_a.csv. The FROZEN entry recovery, the two-way wick cross-check (7.3×10−12), and the MM-raid classification (29 stop-outs → 15 raid-signature) are reused unchanged from backtests/bt_weekly2_stopbuf.py (imported, never modified).
  • Baselines are the ledger itself. Paired-t is against the validated frozen R at 0.05%+1.9R (+0.381994R) and 0.05%+2.5R (+0.505594R) — not a re-simulation. F11 proved the engine reproduces them to ~10−11.
  • Decoupled construction. stop = wick × (1 ± buf), size = $100 / wide_stop_dist, TP = entry ± k × stop_dist0 with stop_dist0 the baseline risk unit; refund watches the raw wick; Friday UK hard-close. Declared 6-cell grid: buf {0.5, 0.75, 1.0}% × k {1.9, 2.5}. No tuning.
  • Determinism. Two independent full runs produced md5-identical per-trade ledgers (f15_decoupled = 4533ed86…). Engine backtests/bt_weekly2_stopbuf_decoupled.py (py_compile clean). In-sample only, 2019→2024-12-31, N=91.
