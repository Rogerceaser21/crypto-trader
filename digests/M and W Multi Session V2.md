# Digest: M and W Multi Session V2

## Summary
Defines the **multi-session M/W** — described as the most powerful entry of the weekly setup / 3-day swing: the MM spikes a level in one session (first peak), then returns in a LATER session or day and deliberately falls short of it (second peak), trapping breakeven-hoping traders. Also covers post-M mechanics (level 1 drop, retracement, board meeting), a strong prohibition on counter-trend trading the 200 EMA after level 1, and stop-loss placement.

## Exact rules
- [00:00] The weekly setup has "really only two trade entries, might be three"; the multi-session M/W is one of them and is the most powerful.
- [00:30] Definition: MM hits a level, then repeats it in **another session or another day** (could be next session, session after, or not until the next day). On the repeat they **fall short on purpose** — returning fully would let trapped traders out at breakeven and remove the MM's liquidity.
- [02:01] The market runs a **three-day OR three-level cycle** (not always three levels — can be three days).
- [02:31] Multi-session M/W's are even better when found at the highs/lows of the day or week.
- [02:31]–[03:02] Weekly context: after the false move week beginning (FMWB), price consolidates/retraces back toward the EMAs while the MM accumulates; spikes high/low can occur during this. An M or W forming = "the decision has been made."
- [03:02]–[03:32] Qualification: first peak spiked in one session; price does NOT come back close to that level until the **next session or the session after** → multi-session M/W = the highly profitable trade.
- [03:32] Example: first peak in Asia, second peak at the very start of UK = "the perfect spot for an M formation, almost guaranteeing a three-level drop to follow."
- [03:32]–[04:02] Entry is on the second peak of the M. Then watch the 3 levels play out; ideally they line up with the high/low of the week or day on the opposite side, or break it and create a new one.
- [04:02] Take profit: at the third level + a stopping volume candle → trade done.
- [04:02]–[04:32] Same pattern, different look per timeframe: on 4H an M can be **tweezer tops** (spans 8+ hours); on 1H the same M = peak → retracement → return to a **lower high** with an **inverted hammer**.
- [04:32]–[05:03] The spikes pull away quickly and return "not as close to the first peak." This usually happens when the **50 and 200 EMA have widened** (retail anticipates continuation).
- [05:03] M/W on the 1H chart = most likely a weekly reversal → fairly tight stop, amazing risk:reward. Experienced: hold through the 3-level move; learning: do NOT hold through the board meeting.
- [05:33] "The reversal is imminent if the price rejects the 200 EMA with a hammer" → take some profit there (ALL profit if still learning). **Do not take 200 EMA reversal (counter-trend) trades as a newbie — almost always a false move to jam traders.**
- [05:33]–[07:03] After the M breaks the 50 EMA and level 1 reaches the 200 EMA: a reversal signal at the 200 EMA is only a retracement (200 → back toward the 50; if very bearish, only to the 10 or 20 EMA) before continuation down. Sometimes it barely retraces and goes into a board meeting. Low-probability trade — skip it; instead wait and join the NEXT rise/drop (level 2).
- [07:03]–[07:34] In the board meeting, look for fake-out spikes. **Validation comes only when the candle closes as a hammer** — always wait for candle closes to enter.
- [07:34] Stop losses: below the low of the previous candle from the first spike, or below the **LOD** if it's a W formation; above the previous spike AND above the **HOD** if it's an M formation.
- [07:34]–[08:04] After the M/W completes, the MM immediately shifts price away to damage the trapped side.
- [08:04] A **vector** (the colored candles) = "retail-perceived momentum" — it looks like retail piling in, but isn't.
- [08:04]–[08:34] After the fast move out of the W: the pullback takes out late-but-right longs; the subsequent consolidation gives false security to trapped shorts. **Consolidation = MM's business is done for the day** ("tick of approval" — the drop/rise is done).
- [08:34]–[09:04] Everything above covers only up to level 1. Next level/day: expect a stop hunt then the level-2 rise/drop, repeating over the next two days/levels. Timing can shift — e.g., a session shift can put a trend in Asia even though Asia typically doesn't run trends.
- [09:04]–[09:34] The levels must come with **higher-than-average volume**; a TTC member indicator (name garbled — see mishears) helps identify this.

## Pattern definition attempt
**Multi-session M (bearish; W mirror):**
- Context: weekly setup after FMWB; retracement/consolidation into the EMAs complete; 50/200 EMA visibly widened; ideally at HOW/HOD.
- Peak 1: spike high in session S1 that pulls away quickly.
- Separation: price does not return near peak 1 within S1; the return happens in S2 = next session, session after, or next day. (Best case per example: peak 1 in Asia, peak 2 at UK open.)
- Peak 2: a **lower high** that "falls short" of peak 1 (distance unquantified — "not as close"; invalidation implied if peak 1 is fully retested, since trapped traders would exit at breakeven).
- Trigger: reversal candle ON CLOSE at peak 2 — inverted hammer (1H) / tweezer top (4H) / hammer for W.
- Entry: at/after that closing candle on the second peak.
- Stop: above peak-2 spike AND above HOD (M); below first-spike's previous-candle low or LOD (W).
- Target: ride 3 levels (or 3 days); final TP at level 3 + SVC; partials at board meetings while learning.
- Invalidation: full return to peak-1 level; reversal candle never closes; price at the 200 EMA rejecting with a hammer = imminent reversal → exit trigger, not entry.

## New jargon
- **Multi-session M/W** — the pattern this lesson defines (peaks in different sessions/days).
- **Tweezer tops** — the 4H expression of an M's double peak.
- **Vector** — colored candle = retail-perceived momentum (also in v2 M and W's).
- **Fake-out spikes** — board-meeting spikes validated only by a hammer close.
- **"TBD Exotator"** (garbled) — TTC members' volume indicator.
- **200 EMA rejection** — here as an exit/warning signal; elsewhere (3 Hits lesson) named as the second weekly setup.
- **Area of Interest (AOI)** — on-slide label (never spoken) for the horizontal band at the consolidation/peak level from which the next level-move departs.
