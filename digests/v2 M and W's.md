# Digest: v2 M and W's

## Summary
Core mechanics lesson on what makes a valid M (double top) or W (double bottom) in the MM method: they must occur after a completed 3-level move, ideally at HOD/HOW (M) or LOD/LOW (W), and the "perfect" version requires the market maker to show up three times — (1) a Stopping Volume Candle at the first peak, (2) three trap-candles on the inside-right of the pattern one timeframe down, (3) a break of the 50 EMA. Also introduces imperfect variants: board-meeting M/W (looser criteria) and Final Damage M/W (second peak exceeds the first).

## Exact rules
- [00:00] Look for M's after a level-3 (three-leg) rise, ideally with price at HOW or HOD. Look for W's after a 3-level drop, ideally at LOD or LOW.
- [00:30] The final (third) level often ends with an SVC.
- [00:30]–[01:00] After the SVC, candle wicks at level 3 point in the direction the MM is trapping: upper wicks after a 3-level rise (trapping longs), lower wicks after a 3-level drop (trapping shorts).
- [01:00]–[01:30] Inverted hammer after a 3-level rise → wick shows the direction price will go next (down). Trade only on candle close (retail enters intrabar and gets trapped).
- [02:00] Levels 1–2 move smoothly (trend phase); aggressive/choppy behavior appears at level 3, which leads into the MM accumulation consolidation.
- [03:01]–[03:31] Trend = EMAs pointing into the top-right/bottom-right corner; going into level 3 the EMAs flatten toward horizontal → consolidation before reversal.
- [03:31]–[04:02] Once the M/W forms, the move away from it is aggressive; the correct M/W puts you in profit quickly. The spikes (wicks) of an M/W must pull away quickly — key distinguishing feature.
- [04:02] W on the 15-minute: the first drop's wick must spike back up really fast; the second peak (low) must NOT repeat the level of the initial fast candle; ideally a hammer (or other reversal candle) on the second side.
- [04:32] Wicks must "snatch away" fast = MM trap. Slow pullback = possibly just profit-taking at key levels → not an M/W.
- [04:32]–[05:02] An M/W on the 1-hour chart = a weekly reversal → tight stop loss, high risk:reward. Experienced traders can hold through 3 levels; while learning, do NOT hold through board meetings — take partial profit at each board meeting and move the stop loss.
- [05:02] An M/W is NOT confirmed until it successfully retests. Skip trades that don't meet strict criteria.
- [06:02] After a W plays out (3-level rise), expect chop — that chop is level-3 behavior for the next cycle.
- [06:02]–[06:33] M/W's also form inside board meetings, but they do NOT follow the perfect-M/W criteria below (no inside-right 3 hits required).

**Perfect M criteria (bearish; W is the mirror):**
- [06:33]–[07:33] MM appearance #1 — SVC at first peak: after a 3-level rise (big green vector candles), the SVC is a smaller candle with a very large wick to the top (can have a lower wick too), volume considerably higher than everything around it (comparable to the prior big vectors), closing red or magenta.
- [08:04] Mark the SVC as the "trapped traders" box/zone. Price may re-enter the box a little, but must NOT complete/traverse the whole box. If price cannot get back to that wick candle, the second side forms a lower high → that is your M.
- [09:04]–[10:05] MM appearance #2 — drop down one timeframe from wherever the first peak was found (e.g., 15m → 5m). On the inside-right side of the M you should see the MM show up three times (e.g., three green candles pushing at the level) to trap longs (retail reads it as a bull-flag breakout).
- [10:05]–[10:35] Entry: after the trap spike fails to re-enter the trapped-trader zone, the red candle after the (two/three) green trap candles is the entry for the second side of the M. Expect a 3-level drop to follow.
- [10:35]–[11:05] MM appearance #3 — break of the 50 EMA (with volume). Annie enters BEFORE the 50 EMA breaks; if price moves away but fails to break the 50 EMA with volume, consider cutting the trade.
- [11:05]–[11:35] Definition restated: an M is an M as long as the MM shows up 3 times — SVC, 3 hits on the inside-right side (lower TF), and the 50 EMA break.

**Perfect W (mirror):**
- [11:35]–[12:35] Downtrend with large red candles; SVC closes green or blue with high volume and large lower wick → mark the trapped-trader zone. If price returns and CLOSES inside the zone with solid candles → not the W yet, keep waiting. A wick into the zone that closes back above it = hint that this is the W.
- [12:35]–[13:36] Example was a 1-hour W → drop to 15m: three red candles on the inside-right (retail sees a bear flag, gets trapped short), then the dip into the zone forms the second side, then the 50 EMA breaks.

**Variants:**
- [14:06]–[14:36] Board-meeting M: SVC forms, price pulls away, comes back and cannot CLOSE back above the SVC → price moves away. (Inside-right 3-hits not required.) In a board meeting, direction comes from either investigating the spikes or an M/W of this looser kind.
- [14:36]–[15:06] Final Damage W: second peak closes LOWER than the first peak — MM either spiking the stops of the traders who were right, or collecting more contracts. (Detailed in the FD lesson.)
- [15:06]–[16:37] Imperfect M (no SVC): acceptable if you know you're at level 3 and can count 3 hits to the high. Mark the candle after the large blue up-candle and wait; entry justified by a large bearish engulfing closing through the other side of the zone. Riskier — no confidence until the 50 EMA is broken AND retested (took ~2 days in the example).
- [17:07]–[18:08] Perfect W checklist recap from live chart: final red down-candle = relatively small candle, large bottom wick, very large volume vs. prior; the green candle after it roughly matches that volume; zone never revisited → W.
- [18:08]–[18:38] Entry precision is the LEAST important element — in the example ~7 candles on the second side were all valid entries. What must be right: stop loss and take profit, with a good risk:reward.

## Pattern definition attempt
**M (bearish reversal; W = exact mirror):**
- Context filter: completed 3-leg rise (level 3), price at/near HOD or HOW, EMAs (50/200) flattening from an up-slope.
- Peak 1 = SVC: small-bodied candle, long upper wick, volume >> local average (on the order of the trend's largest vector candles), closes red/magenta. Its range defines the "trapped-trader box."
- Pullback (inside-left of M) — no requirements stated.
- Peak 2: return leg that (a) does not fully traverse or close within the SVC box with solid bodies, (b) sets a lower high than peak 1 ("comes back, but not as close"), (c) on one timeframe lower shows exactly ~3 push candles into the level (the trap), and (d) prints a reversal candle: inverted hammer / bearish engulfing / first opposite-color candle after the trap candles. Wick re-entry into the box that closes back outside is acceptable (and is the hint).
- Confirmation: 50 EMA broken with volume, then successfully retested [05:02], [16:37].
- Invalidation: solid-body close back inside/through the SVC box; peak 2 repeating peak 1's level; failure to break the 50 EMA with volume after the move away (→ cut trade).
- Timeframes: pattern found on 15m or 1H (1H = weekly reversal); trap-count read one TF lower (5m for a 15m pattern, 15m for a 1H pattern).

## Chart-dependent moments
- [08:04] "Price can return into the box a little bit, but you don't want to see it complete that whole box" — the box boundary (SVC full range vs. body vs. wick) and the tolerated re-entry depth are only shown, never quantified. Critical for coding invalidation.
- [09:35]–[10:05] What exactly counts as the MM "showing up three times" on the lower TF (three consecutive same-color candles? three touches of the level?) — demonstrated visually only.
- [15:37]–[16:07] "The candle after that blue candle... that's the one that you'd mark" — which candle defines the zone in the no-SVC variant is a screen gesture.
- [17:37] "Volume... very, very large in comparison to the volume prior" — no lookback window or multiplier for the SVC volume test.
- [04:02] "You don't want it to repeat the level" — the lower-high tolerance (how close is too close) is never given numerically.

## Quantifiability
**3/5** — Every component is nameable and ordered (SVC → lower-high second side → 50 EMA break → retest), but the thresholds that decide edge cases (SVC volume multiple, box re-entry tolerance, "not as close" distance, wick-speed of the pull-away) are all shown on charts rather than stated as numbers.

## New jargon
- **Vector / vector candles** — the large colored (green/red/blue/magenta) high-volume candles; magenta = high-volume candle closing against the move (the SVC coloring comes from an indicator).
- **Trapped-traders box/zone** — the marked SVC range that price must not reclaim.
- **"MM shows up three times"** — SVC + 3 inside-right hits + 50 EMA break; the definitional test of a perfect M/W.
- **Bull flag / bear flag (retail read)** — what the inside-right trap looks like to retail.
