# Week 2 Entries and Exits — Digest

## Summary
Second worked-example week: a weekend with NO stop hunts or M/W (no FMWB trade), a head-and-shoulders whose right side is the tradable W (including a Final-Damage-W read of the middle peak), a 3-level rise into a Core PCE mid-week reversal, and a second demonstration of when to skip a bad-R:R MWR entry. Also covers the economics and psychology of holding trades over the weekend.

## Exact rules

### Weekend / no-FMWB week
- [00:00–01:00] The skipped trade from Week 1 would have danced around entry all weekend and only paid ~2:1 at dead-gap-zone open — after funding fees and frustration, not worth it. Rule of thumb: prefer being flat over weekends.
- [01:30–02:00] Sometimes the weekend box gives NO hint: no stop hunt high or low, no M, no W — just sideways into the dead gap zone. Reason: MM already trapped enough traders before/during the weekend ("probably biased to one level") so no FMWB stop hunt is needed. In that case there is no FMWB trade to take.

### Head and shoulders = W (entry patterns)
- [02:00–02:30] Head and shoulders is part of this method because "the right side of it is just a W formation (or an M if it's the other way)". Traditional TA reading is irrelevant; the right side W is the focus.
- [02:30–03:00] Two entries available: (a) treat the left side + middle peak as a Final Damage W and enter there; or (b) treat the right side peak + higher low as a plain W and enter there. Multiple entries as price wicked into the area of interest repeatedly.

### Real-trend trade (long)
- [03:00–03:30] MWR landed exactly on Core PCE (Dec 1, grey dots on chart): "you're starting to see how magically the market just reverses on news."
- [03:30] This was the 1H timeframe; the entry "would have been a lot more obvious on the 15" — drop to 15m for entries.
- [03:30–04:01] Level 1 broke the 50 EMA with the green candle, ran for the 200 EMA, then retested "around the 50 EMA, which also lined up with the green vector — a very good area for a retest" (= conservative entry; 2 of the 4 retest conditions in confluence).
- [04:01] Target = the high of the week. Rise level 2 broke straight through the HOW → then wait to see whether it retests the HOW and gives level 3, or the move is over and it's time to exit.
- [04:31–05:01] Conservative stop options: under the many sideways candles holding the 50 EMA; or, if worried about a stop hunt, just below the green vector that created rise level 1. "Don't be afraid to have a bit wider of a stop loss to avoid getting stopped out." Target still the HOW.
- [05:01–05:31] Results: aggressive 4:1 (the head's wick was large, so the stop had to clear it), conservative 5.5:1.
- [05:01–05:31] After the HOW is broken and retested, look for the next target — here it ended up being a vector.

### MWR trade: SKIP (bad setup filter)
- [05:31–06:31] "No entry, all bad risk to reward, bad timing, skip it." Reasons: (1) the news-event wick (Core PCE) into the area of interest (a previous unrecovered vector) was very large → the second-peak return implies a very large stop; (2) price sat very close to the 50 EMA → the 50 EMA can't be the target, so it must be a previous unrecovered vector; (3) that vector was also way too close to entry → bad R:R from setup.
- [06:31] By the time price moved away from the wick and formed a lower high for an entry, it was already the weekend.

### Weekend-holding rules
- [06:31–07:02] Holding into the weekend is acceptable only "if your trade is far enough in profit that you can protect it". Taking NEW trades on a weekend is a bad idea unless close to the new market open. "A Friday right after 5pm New York when the market closes — that's a terrible spot to enter a trade."

## Quantifiability
**3/5.** Skip-filters (target too close, wick too large, weekend timing) are stated as reasons but with no numeric thresholds; the H&S-as-W mapping and retest-confluence entry are structurally clear. A 5 needs numbers for "too close", "very large wick", and minimum R:R.

## New jargon (not in glossary)
- **Head and shoulders (TTC reading)** — not traditional TA; the right side is read as a W/M, the middle peak optionally as a Final Damage W.
- **Funding fees** — cost argument against holding perp positions over the weekend.
- **Grey dots** — the chart marker for news events (forexfactory events plotted on the session indicator).
