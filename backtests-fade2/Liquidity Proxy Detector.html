Liquidity Proxy Detector

A keyless stand-in for a liquidation heat-map, built only from candles per Igor’s ruling 11. The idea: the market maker hunts previous wicks (where the crowd parks stops → where liquidations cluster) and previous unrecovered vectors (fast PVSRA candles that were never rebalanced), across the previous week and the current Monday spike-out leg. This page reports how well that proxy reproduces Igor’s own hand-drawn LIQ Levels labels, and whether the levels line up with the frozen 91 fade entries.

Verdict — honest read

The detector is built, fully causal, and deterministic — but it does NOT yet reproduce Igor’s LIQ-Levels labels, and its levels are too dense to act as a sharp confluence filter. Promising raw material, not a finished signal.

1 · What it detects (plain language)

Two families of level, both read only from candles that closed before the query time — no future data, no paid feed.

(a) Wick levels — 1H chart

  • Find swing-wick extremes with k=3 fractal pivots (the exact patterns.find_pivots convention: a bar higher/lower than the 3 bars on each side).
  • Source window: the previous week + the current week so far.
  • A wick stays active until price trades cleanly through it.
  • Clean-break rule (declared): a later 1H candle closes beyond the wick — close > swing-high or close < swing-low. A pierce that closes back inside does not kill the level.

(b) Unrecovered vector zones — 15m chart

  • Take PVSRA vector candles (200% and 150% volume classes, via pvsra.compute_pvsra) inside the Monday spike-out leg + previous week.
  • A zone is a target while it is unrecovered.
  • Recovery rule (declared): price has not traded back through 50% of the vector’s body (body_mid = (open+close)/2) since it printed. A bull vector recovers when a later low ≤ body-mid; a bear vector when a later high ≥ body-mid. On recovery the zone deactivates.
Week anchor. The task pins evaluation to Monday 00:00 UTC, so the module uses a plain UTC-Monday week (Mon 00:00 → next Mon 00:00), not Annie’s 5pm-NY trading week. At Monday 00:00 the current week is empty, so the active set is the previous week’s inventory. Side is measured against the reference price (last 15m close before t): levels above = one liquidity pool, levels below = the other. Module: detectors/liq_proxy.py · API: liq_levels(t, df_1h, df_15m).

2 · Validation vs Igor’s own labels

Ground truth = the LIQ Levels Short (15M) / LIQ Levels Long (15M) columns in tab_Weekends_Move.csv. What those columns actually contain: integer COUNTS, not prices (e.g. Short=7, Long=35), and only 15 weeks in early-2023 are populated (the 2025 rows are blank; the pre-Jan-2023 rows say “Not Avail from this date”). Because they are counts, the score is a count-direction test: does the proxy agree on which side holds more liquidity at Monday 00:00 UTC?

The side-convention is undocumented — this halves-or-doubles the score. Igor’s columns don’t say whether “Short” liquidity sits above price (where shorts get liquidated) or below it (a short trade’s take-profit target). Under Mapping A (Short=above) the proxy agrees on only 27–33% of weeks — worse than a coin flip, which itself is the tell. Flip to Mapping B (Short=below / the trade-target reading) and agreement rises to 60%. The data leans toward Mapping B, but 60% is still a weak match, and the raw counts never line up (his max 35 vs our max ~18). Needs a one-line ruling from Igor on what the column means.

The proxy captures the idea, not his exact bookkeeping.

3 · Usefulness preview — frozen 91 fade entries

Against backtests/f3_exits_ledger_a.csv, evaluated causally at each real entry time (2019–2024). Two numbers, no optimization — plus one honest control.

84.6%of entries (77/91) had an active liq level within 0.35% — the would-be 4th-retest confluence
56.0%of 1.9R take-profit levels (51/91) sit within 0.35% of an active liq level — target alignment
65.8%saturation floor: mean share of a ±5% window around price already covered by the levels’ 0.35% bands
Read these against the floor. Because the proxy prints ~13–20 levels a week, their 0.35% bands already blanket ~66% of the price neighbourhood. So the 84.6% entry-coincidence is only ~19 points above chance, and the 56.0% TP-coincidence is actually below the floor. Net: the proxy levels are dense, not selective — coincidence with entries/targets is largely saturation. To become a real confluence gate the set must be pruned to a handful of high-conviction levels (e.g. 200%-only vectors, twice-tested wicks) and re-validated.

4 · Worked example — week of 2023-03-13

Weekend label 2023-03-11 → reversal Monday 2023-03-13 00:00 UTC, reference price $21,986 (last 15m close of Sunday). This was a violent risk-on week (SVB / USDC de-peg reversal); BTC ran from ~$20k toward ~$28k.

  • Active set at Monday 00:00: 17 levels — 12 from 1H wicks, 5 from unrecovered 15m vectors.
  • Proxy imbalance: full 6 above / 11 below; 15m-vectors 0 above / 5 below — a clean long-side (below-price) lean, because the prior week left a stack of unrecovered up-vectors underneath as price began to rip higher.
  • Igor’s label: Short 7 / Long 35 — an overwhelming long-side lean.
  • Result: the proxy and Igor agree on the dominant side (both long-heavy under Mapping A). The direction is right; the magnitude is not — his 35 vs the proxy’s 11 below — exactly the scope gap described above.

This is the best-case week: a strong one-directional move leaves an unambiguous vector stack, and the proxy reads it correctly. The misses cluster on quiet/range weeks where the imbalance is small and noise flips the sign.

5 · Proof & reproducibility

  • Module: detectors/liq_proxy.py — reuses detectors/pvsra (vector classes) and detectors/patterns.find_pivots (k=3 fractals). python -m py_compile clean.
  • Causal: every path slices candles to index < t; fractal pivots are only returned once their k confirming bars have closed, so no look-ahead.
  • Deterministic: two independent full passes wrote byte-identical active-level dumps — md5 = 29c2a2cef9639027ee67b876a9f5edd8 (both runs).
  • Data: cached perp candles (2023 era for validation; full 2019–2024 15m + 1H-resampled-from-1m for the usefulness preview). Keyless throughout — no paid liquidation feed.
Bottom line for Igor. The keyless wick + unrecovered-vector idea is sound and now exists as a causal, deterministic module. As tuned it’s too dense to match your sparse hand-counts or to gate entries. Two unblockers: (1) tell me what your “LIQ Levels Short/Long” columns mean — above price, or a short’s target below? (2) say how many levels you actually want per week, so I can prune to the high-conviction few and re-score.
