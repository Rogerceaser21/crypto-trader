Should a peak need 1% beyond the box? — ❌ No

Verdict: requiring a bigger spike destroys the edge, monotonically. At Igor's proposed 1%: trades 167→128, expectancy +0.347R→+0.195R, total +57.95R→+25.01R, significance t 2.84→1.50 (below the 2.0 bar — no longer a validated edge). The 40 dropped weeks were a +21.3R profitable book, and 14 surviving weeks get WORSE entries (−12.6R) because the commit fires later. Third independent confirmation that small pokes fade best (F8, G2, now this). The validated 0.10% stays.

Why it fails twice over. (1) The filter throws away winners: the 40 weeks whose spike stayed between 0.10% and 1% earned +21.3R in the baseline — small pokes are the BEST fades, exactly what the SD-gate test (G2) found on live entries. (2) It poisons the survivors: waiting for the running extreme to clear 1% means the machine commits later, so 14 of the remaining weeks enter on a worse bar — another −12.6R. Bigger-spike-only is a double tax.
