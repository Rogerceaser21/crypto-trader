# Week 4 Entries and Exits — Digest

## Summary
Final worked-example week (FOMC week): a weekend spike WITHOUT an M (only tradable via heatmaps — or via the 50-EMA retest as the second-chance FMWB entry), a W in a vector zone whose level 1 broke the 50 EMA without volume (and why that does NOT invalidate a second-peak entry), the pre-news extension (a 4th level beyond level 3 into major news), and the post-news short where the news vector becomes the area of interest price must not reclaim.

## Exact rules

### Week map
- [00:00–00:30] Spike high out of the weekend trap → W formation over (approximately) Asia into UK → three level rises → a 4th rise = pre-news extension into FOMC (Wednesday Dec 14) → MWR at FOMC mid-week. "This is a typical week."
- [00:30] Rule restated: "sometimes after level three we can get an extension, particularly if it's into a major news event."
- [01:00] "The news is absolutely used as the excuse to move the market regardless of what the result is." Every reversal date lined up with a news date.

### FMWB entries
- [01:30–02:01] A weekend spike that gives NO M formation (just spike-and-fall-away) is a difficult entry; it CAN be traded using heatmaps (what's on the heatmap at the time) — advanced, not part of this course.
- [02:01–02:31] Without heatmaps, the 50-EMA retest is the second opportunity to enter the FMWB: many candles could not get back above the 50 EMA — "pick whichever one you want and just have your stop loss clear the 50 EMA and clear any wicks". Result 3.5:1.

### Real trend (W in a vector zone) — the no-volume level 1
- [02:31] The W formed right in a vector zone — "a perfect spot to be looking for a W".
- [02:31–03:01] Level 1 was NOT perfect — it didn't break the 50 EMA with volume. But when entering on the second peak W you cannot know that in advance; you enter anticipating the volume break.
- [03:01–03:31] Management rule: if you took the aggressive second-peak entry and level 1 breaks without the required volume, your ONLY exit condition is "if price came back below the second peak of the W. Other than that, you don't exit the trade... your reason for entering is only invalidated if price comes back below the second peak W." (Here it went on to give levels 2 and 3.)
- [03:31–04:32] Conservative entry: despite the no-volume level 1, the retest created a higher low → changed market structure (higher highs + higher lows) → bounced off the 50 EMA with a reversal candlestick confirmation → enter there. Same area of interest (the red vector) as target for level 1 only.

### Initial-target rule
- [04:02–05:02] "Your initial target has to be only to your level one target and that has to be the good risk reward, anything after that is cream on top... there's no guarantee you're always going to get three levels. At worst case, you want your level one to be a good enough risk reward for you to enter."
- [05:02] Results: aggressive 4.2:1, conservative 4.2:1 — don't skip the week if you miss the M/W; the 50-EMA retest (or any retest condition) is "potentially still a really good trade".
- [05:32–06:03] Final outcome: holding the 3 levels on the 1H into the news = 16:1; running it into the extension (not advised) = 20:1. One such trade "could make up for a whole month's worth of trades on another system".

### Post-news short (MWR execution)
- [06:03–06:33] The exact FOMC entry was potentially a heatmap setup (advanced); otherwise wait to trade post-news after the price action settles.
- [06:33–07:03] The vector created ON the news becomes the area of interest that price must NOT return to if MWR is on. Price stayed under the 50 EMA for a long period after the news → "any of those candles there could have been your trigger to short with your stop loss above the 50 EMA".
- [07:03] Then ride drop level 2 and level 3, "to be out by the end of UK" (Friday).
- [07:03–07:33] Don't try to outsmart the system with hindsight ("but it kept going below that — you don't know that; this is just a week the US decided to continue the trend; I've shown you Friday traps where the US reversed it").
- [07:33–08:03] "By Friday, you had your three level drops by the end of UK — take your profit and run." Result 4.6:1; the LOW target was hit only later in the weekend.
- [08:03] Decision rule at Friday UK close if target not yet hit: close at the (still amazing) current R:R, or consciously accept the risk of holding through US/weekend knowing US might reverse — a discretionary trader's decision.

## New jargon (not in glossary)
- **Pre-news extension** — a 4th level beyond level 3 that runs into a major news event.
- **News vector** — the vector candle created by the news release; becomes the no-return area of interest for MWR continuation.
- **Cream on top** — profit beyond the initial (level-1) target.
- **Changed market structure** — higher highs + higher lows confirming trend after a W (or inverse for M).
