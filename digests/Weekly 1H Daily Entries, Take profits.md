# Weekly 1H Daily Entries, Take profits — Digest

## Summary
The capstone live session (first ~3 min is intro music): a full live walkthrough of her daily 1H charting routine — marking 1H vectors, the weekend trap box, HOW/LOW, the peak-formation level count, Hyblock liquidation levels, heatmap orders and moon cycles — followed by the complete trade-management ruleset: initial take-profit at the level-1 vector (imbalance logic), the stop-loss movement rules, the refund zone, and the advanced position-building / backflip hedging system. She calls it the de-facto checklist video for the whole method.

## Exact rules

### Daily charting routine (1H, in order)
- [03:41–04:12] Prior lesson marked levels monthly → weekly → daily → (in-between) → 4H; this lesson continues on the 1H, the weekly-setup timeframe.
- [04:42–06:12] On the 1H, scan LEFT for any unrecovered 1H vectors above/below price that sit closer than your higher-timeframe vectors. Note wicks that pierced candidate zones ("this wick went all the way to the top — if there is a vector there, it's pierced it"). She scrolls ~3 months back before concluding none exists [06:12].
- [06:12–07:12] If no 1H vector exists, the higher-timeframe vectors stand — "the higher the timeframe, the more volume in that time period" (HTF vectors outrank).
- [07:12–08:12] Below price, a small unrecovered remainder of a vector close to price "would act as potentially an initial low of the day, being the fact that I'm about to be in the Asia session" — timing context: in Asia, expect the initial LOD to be set. If it doesn't hold, drop to the next vector candle (its wick) and readjust the area of interest [08:12].
- [08:42–09:13] After marking 1H zones, zoom out to the prior weekend and review everything since — later price action can reveal that what you counted as three rises "was actually one".
- [09:13–09:43] "No matter what day of the week you're charting, the first initial step on the one hour timeframe is to mark your weekend trap box."

### Weekend trap box construction
- [09:43–10:13] Box starts at the candle immediately AFTER Friday's US close.
- [10:13] Box ends at the dead gap zone — "the shaded area right before Asia opens... Dead gap zone is 5pm New York" [11:13].
- [10:13–10:43] Box height: NOT the extreme wicks — draw to "the candle close below price and... where price hit most candle closes above price" (wicks above the close-line are excluded from the box).
- [10:43–11:43] Rationale: FMWB "can happen a few hours before dead gap zone when the market opens", so a spike near that time is FMWB, not weekend-trap price action. The box exists "to find out who got trapped".
- [11:43–12:14] Reading the trap: wick down early weekend = inducing shorts; dramatic red candle = more shorts open; end of weekend they get stopped out and breakout long pending orders above get triggered.
- [12:14–12:44] FMWB is generally followed by a W or M — but NOT a typical one (no SVC, no market maker showing up three times on the inside right side); it's fast — "you're really just looking for the shape of an M or W", like in a board room.

### HOW/LOW and the peak-formation count
- [13:14–14:14] After the FMWB one-day move: check where the HOW and LOW are — "at some point in time, price during the week will make an attempt at one of these two levels".
- [14:14–15:48] To orient the count: zoom out and find the LAST peak formation (high if bearish context, low if bullish). "A peak formation is the highest price point went in a period, or the lowest." It usually creates the HOW/LOW. "If you're ever uncertain where you start your count from, you start it from the last peak formation." ("This is definitely gonna be a test question.")
- [14:44–15:17] Example count from the peak-formation high: drop 1 broke the "15 EMA" (almost certainly 50 EMA) on a news event → deep retrace → drop 2 through the weekend → retrace → FMWB = drop 3.
- [15:48–16:48] Drop 3 landed at the LOW with a textbook SVC: "massive volume spike, tiny candle, tiny body, large wick... off the low of the week."
- [16:48–17:18] On a lower timeframe there would likely be 3 hits to the low inside the return move.
- [17:18] W confirmation: price repeated the level, "didn't release everyone in this wick, and it also didn't come back down lower than the low of the week — therefore that's your W formation." Then: broke 50 EMA with volume → retracement → rises.
- [17:48–18:50] Roadblock levels: before assuming the level-3 target, check what's in the way — yesterday's HOD (28,650 here), LOD, HOW (28,900). If entering late, the target must be the nearest roadblock (the HOD), and you must weigh whether that R:R is worth it.

### External confluence (Hyblock, heatmap, delta)
- [19:20–20:21] Check Hyblock liquidation levels: the highest one above price coincided with the HOW + the bottom of the HTF vector — confluence that the count is right.
- [20:21–20:51] "Just a side note — I never trade level three." She'd rather skip the small level-3 move and prepare for the bigger reversal; an active trader may trade level 3, then on an SVC "backflip it and take a short back down".
- [21:23–21:54] Check the delta (Hyblock): "somewhere under nine billion... there's room for upside, but it's getting a little bit thin" — liquidation data consistent with one more rise before a retrace.
- [21:54–23:25] Heatmap: zoom out, find standout orders. - [23:55–24:25] In Asia, look for a high [liquidation/heatmap] level ABOVE and BELOW price — hints where the initial HOD/LOD range will form and prevents FOMO entries in Asia "before enough time has passed".
- [24:55–25:26] Mark key liquidation levels below price too (27,833 here).
- [25:26–26:57] Building the day's conditional trade: IF price retraces to the 50 EMA AND recovers ~50% of the level-2 vector (draw the 50% line of the candle from wick bottom to top) — which coincides with the liquidation level → 4 confluences (vector, 50% of vector, liquidation level, 50 EMA) → long back to the HOD = 4.6:1. Versus front-running the HOW from current price = 1.87:1 — "not worth it".
- [26:57–27:58] If no retracement: let level 3 complete into the zone, look for an SVC, expect the reversal on news. Forexfactory check: Final GDP today (market mover), unemployment "hit and miss", Core PCE tomorrow (big market mover) → these are the MWR candidates for the next two days.

### Her checklist recap (verbatim order)
- [28:28–29:29] (0) Mark the weekend trap box; if the weekend is relevant, figure out where the fake move was. (1) Find the last peak formation high/low — it usually creates the HOW/LOW. (2) Start your level count from it. (3) Count finished? → look for the reversal (W/M). Count mid-way? → identify where level 3 might take you.
- [29:29–29:59] Level definitions while counting: broke the 50 with volume = level 1. A retracement or board meeting that tests an EMA, then shoots out = start of level 2. Pauses WITHOUT board meetings/retracements do not split a level ("all of that is level two").
- [29:59–30:30] In a board meeting, ask: do I want a retracement entry? Is the target good enough from here? ("No, because it's shitty" → no trade.) If only one rise remains: plan the reversal — news event, liquidation levels, high-level heatmap orders.
- [30:30] "For anybody who ever asked for a cheat sheet... watch this video and write it down yourself."

### Moon cycles
- [30:30–31:30] In the market-sessions indicator settings, enable moon cycles. Pay most attention to NEW moon and FULL moon; there is a 3-day buffer around them. The example new moon led to a dramatic downside move and a 3-level drop.
- [31:30–32:00] Sideways 3-level drop: levels do not have to make price progress — level 1 and level 3 can end at the same price ("not all rises and drops have to increase or decrease in price. They can go sideways").
- [32:00–32:30] Full moon comes 14 days after the new moon (use the ruler tool to count days ahead). Minor moons (first quarter etc.) only matter for managing trades — "we don't worry about them".

### Take-profit logic (level 1) and the imbalance
- [33:32–34:32] After a W entry, the FIRST target = the vector candle above — specifically the one before/created-by the "very fast red candle" whose speed left an imbalance.
- [34:32–36:33] Imbalance mechanics: a large fast vector candle wipes both sides of the order book in one go; nobody places stops (and hence no TPs) inside that zone, so the book stays empty there "until consolidation happens in that area" after price returns. A fast move back through it does NOT fill it.
- [37:04–37:34] Verification tool: TradingView "Fixed Range Volume Profile" from the peak before the move to the peak after — the imbalance shows as a "massive little crevice" in the profile.
- [38:04–38:35] Expectation chain: level-1 rise = fast move crossing the 50 EMA with volume (won't fill the imbalance) → consolidation likely follows AS the 50-retracement, which fills the imbalance before level 2. That's why the initial TP is set at that vector.
- [39:05–39:35] Trade skeleton: enter at the close of the second-peak candle; stop below the first wick that created the W; TP at the level-1 vector.
- [39:35–40:36] "Your initial target is only to level one" even though you expect 3 levels — "a W is not confirmed until it retraces after the right side of the W", and a second-peak entry is taken BEFORE confirmation.
- [40:36–41:07] Put a price alert at the level-1 target; when it fires, check: rise 1 complete? no SVC? all normal? → hold longer.

### Stop-loss movement rules (the hard rules)
- [41:07–41:37] Move stop to break-even ONLY after the retracement has happened AND level 2 has started ("only after, guys, only after").
- [41:37–42:38] Never move the stop before the initial target is hit. When you do move it, "you cannot move it lower than your initial target" — otherwise a trade you sized at your minimum R:R (she cites 1.9) can resolve as 1:1 and corrupts your trade journal/statistics.
- [42:38–44:08] If price moved up, retraced, and moved away without hitting the initial TP: stop goes to break-even exactly — "there's nothing in between" entry and the initial TP.
- [43:08–43:38] Board meetings can include a stop hunt before level 2 — don't trail the stop too tight into a board meeting.
- [44:08–44:39] Once level 2 is underway: stop may go above the initial TP only if structure allows; otherwise it stays at entry.
- [47:40–48:10] Level-2 target selection: from the current candle, find the next UNRECOVERED candle above price; skip candidates that already have wicks into them; if the next one is "too high to be a level-2 target", use the unrecovered remainder of a closer vector. (Extended the trade to 3.2:1, risk-free with stop at BE [48:10].)
- [48:41–49:41] Protecting profit during level 2: the stop still can't go below the initial TP; leave room for the expected retracement into your marked liquidity level; her personal line — "if price came below the 50 EMA at this point in time, I don't wanna be in this trade anymore" → stop just under the initial TP/50 EMA.
- [49:41–50:42] If you're not willing to give back unrealized profit waiting for level 3 — take the profit. No stop-jockeying, no greed, no "I took profit too early" regret.
- [50:42–51:12] At level 3, if an SVC prints: "at the very least take some profit there."
- [51:12–51:42] Every stop-move point is equally a partial-close point (close a percentage of contracts in OKX) — recommended while learning.

### Refund zone
- [51:42–52:42] Refund zone is ONLY relevant when entering on a second-peak M/W. It is where you cut the trade at break-even (or just transaction costs) because the M/W has become invalid — instead of riding it to the full stop.
- [53:12–54:13] Invalidation trigger: a candle CLOSES below the wick of what you believed was the second peak W (above, for an M). "A second peak of the W or M should not return to that level ever again."
- [54:13–55:13] "What if it ends up being a Final Damage W? You're gonna be stopped out anyway before you know that" — and after the FD stop hunt you re-enter lower with a better R:R that "will almost immediately make up for that tiny little loss".
- [55:13–56:44] The refund zone is logic, not fear: cut ONLY on the close-based invalidation. Traders who only enter after the 50-EMA volume break never need a refund zone — their stop simply goes below the board-meeting stop hunt or below the W/M formed in the board meeting.

### Building positions (advanced — hedged backflip execution)
- [57:14–57:44] Baseline sizing: 1% account risk per trade (full risk-management lesson recorded separately).
- [59:15–59:45] Prerequisite: "no one should be doing that until they have at least six months or a hundred trades under their belt with a good win rate and a good minimum risk to reward."
- [58:14–59:15] Why build: large accounts using market orders get bad fills / partial limit fills; splitting orders gets everything filled at better average prices.
- [59:45–61:16] Split the position into 3–4 orders. Order #1 = 25% of size, placed immediately AFTER the stopping volume candle, stop under its wick.
- [61:46–63:48] The hedge logic: you're still holding the short from the 3-level drop (e.g. 100 contracts short vs 25 long). If the SVC fails and price drops further, the 25-contract loss is made back 4x by the open short. "I'm basically hedged both ways" while waiting to see if the reversal is real.
- [64:18–65:49] Adds #2–3: as price returns to build the inside right side of the W, drop a timeframe and watch the 3 hits to the low; add on hits PROVIDED the candles do not close into the first wick area (here: "they didn't close into this wick area... this one closed above"). If they start closing into the wick area — stop adding.
- [65:49–66:19] Final add: either after the next move that proves the W, or on the break-and-retest of the 50 EMA when the W is confirmed (case-by-case).
- [67:20–67:50] Full confirmation: price returned and did NOT close back above those 15m closes → fully positioned long, EXIT the short (banked ~4.8:1).
- [68:20–68:51] The perpetual backflip: hold the W long through 3 levels → M forms → start building shorts at the SVC and on the 3 hits to the high that create the second peak M → "that's how you're always in a trade, backflipping from rises to drops with no downtime."
- [68:51–69:51] Her own practice: rarely trades the daily setup; the weekly 1H is the job — "my number one goal every week is get myself that peak formation entry and hold it for three levels." Two entries a week ≈ 5:1 + 5:1.

## New jargon (not in glossary)
- **Weekend trap box** — precise construction: candle after Friday US close → dead gap zone, drawn to candle-close clusters, wicks excluded.
- **Dead gap zone** — the pre-Asia lull; stated flatly as "5pm New York" [11:13] (glossary's "Dead Zone").
- **Peak formation** — highest/lowest price point of a period; the mandatory anchor for level counting.
- **Roadblock** — intervening level (HOD/LOD/HOW/LOW) that caps a target.
- **Imbalance** — order-book void left by a fast vector candle; filled only by consolidation.
- **Fixed Range Volume Profile** — TradingView tool used to verify imbalances.
- **Initial target / initial TP** — the level-1 vector target that anchors all stop-movement rules.
- **Refund zone** — close-based early-exit of a second-peak entry at ~break-even.
- **Building positions** — 3–4 tranche entry (25% at SVC, adds on 3 hits, final on confirmation) hedged by the still-open opposite trade.
- **Moon cycles** — new/full moon reversal windows with a 3-day buffer; minor moons ignored.
- **Sideways three-level drop** — 3 levels that make no net price progress.
- **Delta (Hyblock)** — liquidity/liquidation metric she checks (~$9B here); exact definition unclear.
- **Front run** — entering ahead of an obvious level (HOW) at degraded R:R.
