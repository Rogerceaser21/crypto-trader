Verdict — how mechanizable is this, honestly?

Fade 2 is a crisp scaffold wrapped around a discretionary core. Everything that is a number — the session clock, PVSRA volume classes, the EMA stack, the SVC shape, the Friday exit — is codeable today and already coded. Everything that is a reading — which move is the "fake" one, which wick is a valid M/W, which way it breaks, and how many levels have printed — is where Annie herself says you will "feel confused for a very long time," and it is where our detectors land near a coin-flip against Igor's own labels. A fully deterministic Fade-2 detector is not achievable from the material as written; a scaffold + calibrated-proxy hybrid (fire on the crisp gates, score the fuzzy core, let Igor's labels tune the thresholds) is.

Micro-ruleTagSource
The market maker "shows up three times" to build an M/W: (1) the SVC at peak 1 stops the prior momentum; (2) three trap candles on the inside-right of the formation; (3) the candle that finally breaks the 50 EMA. SOURCED"v2 M and W's" ~[10:35–11:35]
To see the three trap candles you drop down one timeframe from where the first peak was found: a 15m M → read the inside-right on the 5m; a 1H M/W → read it on the 15m. SOURCED"v2 M and W's" ~[09:04–09:35],[12:35]
The three trap candles are three consecutive same-colour candles pushing into the level (green under an M to trap longs; red under a W to trap shorts) — they mimic a bull/bear-flag breakout. SOURCED"v2 M and W's" ~[09:35–10:05],[13:06]; rulebook §2

Entry cue = the first opposite-colour candle after the three trap candles (e.g. the red candle after two/three green under an M). SOURCED"v2 M and W's" ~[10:05]; rulebook §2 (✓frames)

Level anatomy — L1 / L2 / L3 (step 5 in depth)

Primary sources: "The Weekly Setup V2" ~[03:31–10:35]; "v2 M and W's" ~[02:00–03:31]; "v2-VSC and Volume" ~[01:31–04:02]; "v2-Board Meetings"; "Week 3" ~[04:03–05:03]; rulebook §1–2 + rulings #2, #4.

What counts as "one level"

  • SOURCED A level is not one candle — "a level can be three or four candles … all of this is one level" because it happened around the same time; a lone spike is its own level. "v2-VSC" ~[01:31]
  • SOURCED Read levels off the volume sub-pane: one contiguous burst of ~4–6 elevated coloured bars = one level. rulebook §2 (✓frames)
  • SOURCED Start the level count from the last peak formation (the M/W), not from an arbitrary point. rulebook §1; "S3"
  • MISSING The exact "same time / same cluster" gap that merges bars into one level vs starts the next is not numerically defined. see Q5

Level 1 — volume-spike cluster, then consolidation/channel

  • SOURCED L1 = the vector cluster that breaks the 50 EMA with volume, then runs toward the 200 EMA and either goes into a board meeting or a retracement. "Weekly Setup V2" ~[04:01–04:31]
  • SOURCED Initial take-profit = the origin of the L1 vector (order-flow logic; cross-check with a Fixed-Range Volume Profile low-volume "crevice"). Your L1 target alone must already be a good R:R. rulebook §1–2; "Week 4" ~[04:02–04:32]
  • SOURCED #1 prohibition: never counter-trend after L1 — not even on a 200-EMA reversal candle. That 200-EMA bounce after L1 is a retracement trap, not the "200-EMA rejection" setup. "Weekly Setup V2" ~[05:02–05:32]; "Multi Session" ~[05:33–07:03]

Level 2 — break with PVSRA candles, then consolidation

  • SOURCED L2 target = the 800 EMA, or a previous unrecovered vector if the 800 is not in reach; both together = strong confluence. "Weekly Setup V2" ~[07:33–08:03]
  • SOURCED Between L1 and L2 (and L2–L3) you expect a board meeting or a retracement; in a very strong trend these can be skipped entirely. "Weekly Setup V2" ~[07:33],[08:03]
  • SOURCED Retail breakout/pattern trades work at L1/L2 and fail at L3. rulebook §1

Level 3 — the strongest leg, "where most people get liquidated"

  • SOURCED Explicit leverage-cascade story: "A level 1 drop will generally liquidate 100x traders, level 2 the 50x, level 3 the 25x." Below 25x is "generally safe unless the move is extremely extended." "Weekly Setup V2" ~[03:00–03:31]; "v2-Liquidation levels" ~[02:31–03:01] — this is Igor's "where most people get liquidated." Note: it is a rationale, not a mechanical input (we have no per-trader leverage feed).
  • SOURCED L3 is often less dramatic than L1/L2 — "more like sideways price action," patience-testing, and the EMAs start to flatten (fan-out gone). It leads into the accumulation/consolidation before the reversal. "v2 M and W's" ~[02:00–03:31]
  • SOURCED At L3 the candle wicks switch sides (now trapping the other way) and you look for the SVC first — that is the "levels are in, reversal coming" hint. "Weekly Setup V2" ~[10:04–10:35]; "v2 M and W's" ~[00:30–01:30]
  • SOURCED Best L3 target stacks: a higher-timeframe 200/800 EMA + an unrecovered vector + a prior HOW/LOW/HOD/LOD + a liq level, all lined up. "Weekly Setup V2" ~[08:03–10:04]
  • SOURCED "Damage is done" shortcut: a near-vertical move + an extended board meeting means no more levels are owed — the three levels may have completed on the 15m instead of the 1H. rulebook §1; "Week 3" ~[04:03–05:03]

Board meeting / channel (the pause between levels)

  • SOURCED A board meeting is just a channel (Igor ruling #4) — usually sideways, can slope up/down, can retrace back to the breaking PVSRA candle. No special boundary-drawing rules exist. ruling #4; "v2-Board Meetings"
  • SOURCED Consolidation = "a tick of approval — they just hurt someone and the move is done for now." Read the wicks inside it: longer wicks on one side show who is being trapped (the stop-hunt board meeting). "Multi Session" ~[08:34]; "Weekly Setup (Week 1)" ~[08:33–09:33]
  • SOURCED Management: take partials at each board meeting, move stop to breakeven only after L2 starts, never trail below the initial TP. rulebook §2 risk template
  • MISSING No numeric definition of "sideways enough / long enough" to be a board meeting. see Q7

SVC — the leg-ending signal (ruling #2)

  • SOURCED Shape is fixed: small body, long wick (either side), body smaller than the wick, on a volume spike — a "stopping volume candle." Size of the spike is subjective; its example SVCs are 150%(blue)-class, ~3–5× neighbouring volume. ruling #2; "v2-VSC" ~[04:02]; "v2 M and W's" ~[06:33–07:33]
  • SOURCED Under 1H, an engulfing candle (150%+ PVSRA) also qualifies as the stopping signal — and this improves agreement with Igor's "Reversal SVC" labels by +9pp (to 61%). ruling #2; CALIBRATION_V2
  • SOURCED The tell that it is really L3 (not L2): after the SVC, price fails to get back into the SVC's week. If price returns into it, "the damage hasn't been done yet." "v2-VSC" ~[02:31–03:01]
  • SOURCED A blue candle at the end of a rise is a pause / guaranteed break, not a guaranteed reversal. "v2-VSC" ~[03:31–04:02]

Open questions for Igor

Every ambiguity that blocks a mechanical detector, each with a concrete proposed default so a "yes" ships it and any other answer overrides it. (Question 0, the abort rule, is tracked separately.)

  1. Is the 50-EMA vector-cross required to ENTER, or only to COUNT L1? Igor says "cross preferred, NOT required"; the rulebook says "L1 must break the 50 with a vector or it doesn't count." These are reconcilable but the detector needs one rule. Proposed default: the aggressive second-peak M/W entry does not wait for the cross (enter on peak 2, invalidate only on a close beyond peak 2); the 200%-vector 50-EMA cross is required only to count L1 for the three-level hold and is the conservative-entry confirmation. Cross gates the count, not the entry.
  2. Which direction(s) may Fade 2 fire? Detector direction is a coin-flip (50%). Do we let it fire both ways and let geometry decide, or constrain it? Proposed default: Fade 2 only trades the reversal of the Monday false move, and the M/W must sit at the weekly extreme — W at the LOW → long, M at the HOW → short. No naked both-sides firing.
  3. How is the weekend box built, exactly, and what counts as a "spike out"? "Close-clusters not wicks" is descriptive. Proposed default: box = min/max of candle closes in the Fri-17:00-NY → Sun-17:00-NY window; a fake move = a wick beyond the box that closes back inside within 3 candles (15m). If no such spike, fall back to an M/W inside the box.
  4. Confirm the peak-2 "vicinity" bound at 35%? Grid-chosen on your weeks (33% trigger-exact agreement; ties broke tighter). Proposed default: peak-2 shortfall/overshoot up to 35% of the internal leg is a valid M/W; beyond 35% = breakout, not a fade. Keep 35% until a bigger labelled set moves it.
  5. What merges bars into "one level" vs starts the next? Annie: 4–6 elevated bars can be one level; a lone spike is its own. Proposed default: a contiguous run of PVSRA-coloured bars (200% or 150%) with no gap of ≥6 below-average-volume bars between them = one level; a ≥6-bar quiet gap starts the next level's count. Tune the 6 on the labelled weeks.
  6. When does the level count live on 15m instead of 1H (the "damage is done" shortcut)? Proposed default: count on the 1H by default; if a single 1H leg is "vertical" (its range ≥ 3× the trailing-20 1H candle range) and is followed by a board meeting of ≥8 bars, switch the remaining count to the 15m. Confirm the 3× / 8-bar knobs.
