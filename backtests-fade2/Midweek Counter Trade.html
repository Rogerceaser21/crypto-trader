The Midweek Counter Trade

Igor: “we’ve found that Wednesday and Thursday have a peak — there must be a way we can utilise this to have an edge.” This page hunts that edge as a separate, standalone trade: when a midweek M/W (or Final Damage) reversal completes on the 1H, we trade the direction the pattern implies and see if it pays. It does not touch the Monday fade. Verdict up top, honest priors, the full declared grid, and the drift table that explains the result.

Verdict · No edge

The midweek peak is a real EVENT — but it is not tradeable against.

All 6 declared cells lose money, every one of the 6 years is negative, and the t-stats are significantly negative (−2.1 to −4.2) — not weakly-zero, but reliably against the counter-trade. The drift table shows why: after the signal fires, price on average keeps moving against the pattern direction at every horizon. Countering the midweek peak means fighting the dominant drift. Ship nothing. The tradeable direction here is with the prior move (continuation) — which is exactly what F7 already told us.

1 · Honest priors, declared before the run

Two prior results pointed hard at a null before a single trade was simulated. We state them, then test against them.

a
The standalone midweek entry already died once. In the original campaign, the S4 “Mid-Week Reversal” standalone entry went 0 / 12. A naked midweek-reversal trigger had no track record to build on.
b
F7 proved price CONTINUES the fade after this exact signal. Exiting the Monday fade at the midweek reversal underperformed simply holding to 2.5R — meaning, on average, price keeps going the fade’s way past the signal. The fade direction is the opposite of what the midweek pattern implies, so prior (b) argues directly against a counter-trade.

Pre-registered decision rule: if all 6 cells are negative or insignificant, the verdict is “no edge” and we say so plainly. A cell is promoted to candidate only if it clears t ≥ 2.5 (flagging that we ran 6 comparisons); only then do we run Monte Carlo + a 3-perturbation sensitivity. No cell qualified, so neither was run.

2 · What the trade is

Entry signal (reused verbatim from F7)

  • Detector V2 (M/W + Final Damage) on the 1H, the single declared detector — no shopping.
  • Peak-2 is a k=3 fractal pivot, only knowable k bars later; the causal confirmation bar is b = peak2 + 3. Nothing uses future data.
  • Completion must be Wed 00:00 NY or later and before the Friday-UK cap.
  • Scan both a top reversal and a bottom reversal; take whichever completes first (one trade / week).
  • Direction the pattern implies: M-top → SHORT, W-bottom → LONG.

Trade construction

  • Entry = open of the next 15m bar after the 1H confirmation bar closes.
  • Stop = beyond the pattern’s peak (peak-2 extreme) ±0.05% — above for a short, below for a long.
  • Target = a fixed R multiple: 1.0R / 1.5R / 1.9R.
  • Final exit = Friday UK-session close (hard cap).
  • Fills: 1m intrabar, same-bar stop&target ambiguity = loss, fees 0.05%/side, slip 0.02%/fill, 1R = $100 fixed.

Detection composition (265 signals): 133 shorts / 132 longs · types M 104, W 107, FDM 29, FDW 25 · completion day Wednesday 199, Thursday 58, Friday 8. The Wed/Thu concentration confirms Igor’s observation — the midweek peak is a real, well-populated event. The question is only whether it is tradeable against.

The edge, such as it is, points the opposite way to what Igor hoped for this setup.

Both directions show it (short weeks −0.78% to Friday, long weeks −0.56%). This is the mechanical reason all six trade-construction cells lose: there is no reversal to harvest, only continuation to get run over by.

5 · Honest conclusion

The observation was right; the trade is wrong. The Wednesday/Thursday peak is real and frequent (257 of 265 signals land Wed or Thu) — a genuine calendar feature of the tape. But it is a marker of where the week’s move matured, not a reversal you can sell/buy into. Every attempt to trade against it over 5.3 years of BTC loses, in every year, at every target, on both the full universe and the fade-week subset.
  • No edge — do not build this. The midweek peak is tradeable as an event (it tells you the week’s structure is set), but not as a counter-trade against.
  • Where the edge actually points: the strongly-negative counter-trade t implies a continuation bias (stay with the prior move / hold the fade) — the same conclusion F7 reached from the exit side. If anything is worth a future look, it is a midweek continuation add, not a counter.
  • No promotion. 0 of 6 cells cleared t ≥ +2.5, so no Monte Carlo and no sensitivity were run — there was nothing to stress-test.
