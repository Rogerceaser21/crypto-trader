The five picks

Five candidates, five different edges claimed: trend-following, volatility breakout, squeeze-release momentum, dip-buying in trend, and liquidity-sweep reversal. No two from the same family.

Pick 1Trend Rider — EMA cross with macro filter 4h · long + shortproposed

Library name: C23-4h tunable · author: anonymous · claimed window Mar 2020 → Aug 2024

PF 3.39Sharpe 1.3 net +22% max DD 4.4%56 trades win 30%

The idea: Enter when the 20-bar average crosses the 75-bar average, but ONLY in the direction of the long-term macro trend (a ~200-day average). Stop at 3× the recent bar range (ATR); risk 0.3% of account per trade.

Code review: Cleanest code of the twelve. Fully causal (signals on closed bars, fills next bar open), honest risk-based sizing, no leverage games.

Why it made the five: Best profit factor in the long-window set (3.39). Same family as the ONE strategy that survived our Krown improvement campaign (the daily trend-follower) — so it has a validated prior, and we can compare it head-to-head against that benchmark.

What we strip / watchNothing major. Only 56 trades in 4.4 years — the sensitivity grid must confirm the edge isn't 5 lucky trends. Name says 'tunable' = author iterated parameters; expect some fit.
Pick 2Volatility-Surge Breakout 4h · long + shortproposed

Library name: QL ATR Percentile Surge BTC 4h · author: anonymous · claimed window Mar 2020 → Dec 2024

PF 2.00Sharpe 1.3 net +66% max DD 6.2%191 trades win 47%

The idea: When market volatility (ATR) suddenly jumps into the top 20% of its recent range, ride the move in whatever direction price is leaning (above/below its 20-bar average). Get out when volatility normalises, after 42 bars, or at a 2.5×ATR stop.

Code review: Causal, symmetric long/short, fixed-dollar risk per trade. Simple and fully mechanical.

Why it made the five: A genuinely DIFFERENT mechanism from everything we've tested so far (Annie = weekly structure, Krown = oscillators, C2 = slow trend). 191 trades over 4.8 years = the fattest sample in the top set.

What we strip / watchHis 2-tick slippage assumption is optimistic for surge conditions — our engine's cost model (0.05%/side + slippage, ambiguous fills = losses) will pressure-test exactly that.

We test the entry/exit SIGNAL at honest 1× sizing; the claimed 24% drawdown would be far smaller unlevered — and the return far lower.
Pick 4RSI Dip-Recovery in Uptrend (“Strategy 667”) 15m · long onlyproposed

Library name: 策略667（15分钟优化版·存档） · author: anonymous · claimed window Jul 2020 → Jul 2026

PF 2.96Sharpe 1.0 net +36% max DD 4.0%72 trades win 50%

The idea: In an established uptrend (price above the 300-bar average, ADX trend-strength filter on), wait for RSI to dip below 40, then buy when it recovers back through 45. Chandelier trailing stop + initial 3×ATR stop. Stops trading entirely if the account draws down 12%.

Code review: Causal and clean. From a Chinese-community author (“策略667” = “Strategy 667”) with three variants in the library — this 15m version has the best profit factor.

Why it made the five: The longest claimed window in the whole top set (6 full years, 2020→2026) with PF 2.96 and only 4% drawdown. It's also our only 15m candidate — tests whether ANY lower-timeframe entry survives real costs, the exact place Krown's scalps died.

What we strip / watchTwo fitted quirks we strip/ablate: a forced minimum 50%-of-equity position floor, and the account-drawdown halt (equity-curve feedback). 72 trades in 6 years = very selective; thin sample.
Pick 5Liquidity-Sweep Reversal (Smart-Money style) 1h · long onlyproposed

Library name: BTC/USD Candle Range Theory · author: anonymous · claimed window Jan 2023 → Jun 2025

PF 2.30Sharpe 1.4 net +11% max DD 2.4%51 trades win 45%

The idea: A candle spikes BELOW the prior candle's low by at least 0.3×ATR (a stop-hunt) but closes back inside the prior range — the sweep failed. If the bigger trend is up (above 50-bar average, RSI>50, ADX confirms), buy the failed sweep. Stop 1.5×ATR, target 4×ATR.

Code review: Causal entry on closed bars. The exit code is sloppy (it closes at the NEXT bar after a level is touched instead of at the level — his numbers are approximate). Our engine handles the brackets properly, with ambiguous bars counted as losses.

Why it made the five: Straight from the Smart-Money-Concepts school DaviddTech brands himself on — and a direct cousin of Annie's liquidity-sweep ideas. If it holds up, it cross-validates a concept two of your courses share.

What we strip / watchThinnest sample of the five (51 trades, 2.5 years) and the claimed +11% is modest. This one is here for the CONCEPT test as much as the raw numbers.

Honesty section

Read this before falling in love with any curve above

  • Every number on this page comes from HIS platform. His engine, his fill assumptions, his data. We use them to CHOOSE candidates, never as evidence. A strategy earns its stats on our engine or not at all.
  • Leaderboard = survivorship machine. People iterate strategies on his platform until the numbers look good, then they sit on the leaderboard. Two of our twelve finalists admit this in their own code comments. Expect shrinkage everywhere.
  • The locked out-of-sample window has an asterisk here. His backtest windows run into 2026 — so picking winners from his leaderboard already "peeks" at 2025-26 performance. Our primary evidence for these five will be the causal in-sample test (2019–2024); the eventual one-shot out-of-sample run carries a selection-bias caveat we will state on the scorecard.
  • Expect deaths. Annie: 1 survivor of 7 setups. Krown: 0 of 10. The value is finding the one that lives.
