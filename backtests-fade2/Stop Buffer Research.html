Annie Trading · Monday Fade V1 · Task F11

Stop Buffer Research

You noticed V1 entries getting “stopped out by the market maker because the SL was too close” to the weekly extreme wick. This tests exactly that: how far can the hard stop back off from the wick before the edge dies — and are the baseline stop-outs really MM wick-raids that reverse? Run on the 91 frozen Monday-fade entries (in-sample 2019→2024), entries untouched, only the stop moved.

Backing the stop off does not improve the validated fade on a fixed-R exit — it’s a statistical wash at 1.9R and a net loss at 2.5R. Your MM-raid instinct is half right: ~half the stop-outs genuinely are wick-raids, and a wider stop does rescue them — but the fixed-R geometry hands the gains straight back.

  • Which buffer, if forced: only 0.75% at the 1.9R target holds expectancy (0.382→0.421R, PF 2.05→2.42, drawdown 4.6→3.7R, stop-outs halved 29→15) — but paired t vs the 0.05% baseline is just +0.56, nowhere near the 2.5 significance bar. Treat it as free comfort, not free edge.
  • Cost of going wider: at 2.5R (the F3 winner) expectancy falls monotonically 0.506→~0.33R and the 0.5% cell reaches paired t −2.20. Do not widen the stop at the 2.5R target.
  • Why the wash: a wider stop shrinks position size and pushes the fixed-R TP farther out (1.9R of a bigger distance). The ~+12R recovered from rescued raids at the 1.0% buffer is almost exactly cancelled by winners that no longer reach the farther target (TP hits 32→23 at 1.9R).
  • The real lever the data points to — decouple stop from target (back the stop off to dodge raids but keep TP on the original tight risk unit) — is outside this declared grid. Flagged below as the follow-up test, not claimed here.

Anchor check — the 0.05% cell at 1.9R reproduces the frozen ledger to machine precision: mean +0.381994R (target +0.382), max per-trade R gap 4.8×10−11, exit-type match 91/91. Wick recovered two ways (from frozen stop vs re-derived from candles) agree to 7.3×10−12. Both determinism runs md5-identical.

The catch. A 1.0% buffer recovers +12.70R on the stop-out subset — real money. Yet the full-book total at 1.0% is only 34.2R vs the baseline’s 34.8R. The entire +12.7R rescue is eaten by the rest of the 91 trades: winners whose 1.9R TP moved out of reach (and now settle for a smaller Friday R) plus trades the refund rule caps near the wick. Net-net, the book barely moves. That trade-off is the answer.

4 · MM stop-raid analysis — is the tight stop being hunted?

Yes, for about half the stop-outs — and the data is specific about it. Classifying each of the 29 baseline stop-outs by what the stop bar itself did:

So the phenomenon you saw is real: more than half the stops are textbook wick-raids — price spikes through a stop sitting ~5bp above the wick, then closes back inside the same 15-minute bar, with a median overshoot of only ~0.53% beyond the wick. Diagnosis confirmed, with a boundary. Backing the stop off to 0.75% recovers roughly +10R from the raid-signature subset alone (5 of 15 survive, 3 become net winners) — those really were MM wick-hunts. The genuine-break subset is un-rescuable by stop width: the refund-on-close-through-wick rule ejects every one of them regardless, and not a single one turns profitable even at a 1.0% stop. Your system’s refund rule is already doing the right thing there.
Why widening still doesn’t pay on the headline. The ~+10R you rescue from raids is handed back on the other side of the book because the fixed-R TP scales with the wider stop. The fix implied by the data is not a wider fixed-R stop — it’s to keep the TP on the original tight risk unit (or the vector target) while backing the hard stop off ~0.5–0.75% so intrabar raids miss it. That decouples the two effects and is a clean, declared next test (a new grid), not something claimed by this one.

5 · Method & reproducibility

  • Entries frozen. The 91 Monday-fade entries come straight from backtests/f3_exits_ledger_a.csv (week_start, entry_time, direction, entry_ref, stop, stop_dist). Nothing is re-detected.
  • Wick recovered two ways & cross-checked. (1) from the frozen stop and the 0.05% baseline buffer wick = stop / (1 ± 0.0005); (2) re-derived from candles as bt_weekly does — running high (short) / low (long) over [week-open .. entry). They agree to 7.3×10−12 on all 91.
  • Per-cell trade rebuilt in full. new stop → size = $100 / new_stop_dist → TP = entry ± target × new_stop_dist; refund watches the raw wick; Friday UK-session hard close. Fills verbatim from bt_weekly.execute(): taker fee 0.05%/side, slippage 0.02%/fill, 1-minute intrabar resolution, same-bar stop & target = loss, 1R = $100 fixed risk.
  • Declared grid, no tuning. Buffers {0.05, 0.10, 0.20, 0.30, 0.50, 0.75, 1.00}% plus the ATR variant wick ± 0.5×ATR(14) (Wilder RMA on 15m, causal value at the bar before entry). Two targets: 1.9R and 2.5R. In-sample only, 2019→2024-12-31.
  • Determinism. Two independent full runs produced md5-identical per-trade CSVs (f11_stopbuf_1p9 = 15eb72e8…, f11_stopbuf_2p5 = 1df1c8af…). Engine: backtests/bt_weekly2_stopbuf.py (py_compile clean).
